## 🏛️ The Delhi "Big Four" (Government)

### 1. Maulana Azad Medical College (MAMC)
Connected to the LNJP Hospital, MAMC is arguably the second most prestigious MBBS college in India after AIIMS Delhi. The intensity of practical cases here is legendary.
- **Quota:** 85% seats are reserved for students who did their 11th and 12th from Delhi.

### 2. VMMC & Safdarjung Hospital
VMMC is part of Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University (GGSIPU). Safdarjung Hospital is the largest OPD in Asia, providing students with massive surgery and trauma experience.

### 3. Lady Hardinge Medical College (LHMC)
One of the oldest and most respected medical colleges for women in India. Located in Connaught Place, it is famous for its Pediatrics and Obstetrics focus.

## 🚀 Private Medical Leaders (Noida & Gurgaon)

If you miss the government cutoffs but have the budget, these private/deemed campuses offer world-class infrastructure and high hospital bed capacity:

### 1. Jamia Hamdard (HIMSR), Delhi
A deemed university with a high reputation for research and ethical medical practice. It is one of the few private-style options located within the main Delhi city limits.

### 2. Sharda University (School of Medical Sciences), Greater Noida
Connected to the Sharda Hospital, which serves a massive rural/urban population of UP. It is a preferred choice for students from North India targeting direct admission via secure NEET ranks.

## 🏗️ The Delhi NCR Medical Edge

For a 2026 medical student, Delhi NCR offers:
- **Internal PG Quota:** Graduates from DU colleges (MAMC, LHMC, UCMS) and IPU colleges (VMMC, RML) get a massive advantage in the 50% State Quota for MD/MS admissions.
- **Technological Hubs:** Access to AIIMS’s specialized research centers and corporate diagnostic hubs in Gurgaon for specialized internships.
- **Top Faculty:** Delhi remains the city where most of India’s top-tier doctors and authors of medical textbooks are based.

## 🏗️ Direct MBBS Admission in NCR 2026

Management quota or NRI seats are legally available in private/deemed universities like SGT (Gurgaon) and Sharda (Greater Noida).
- **Process:** You MUST be NEET-qualified. Seats are allocated through the MCC Deemed counselling or State UP NEET counselling.
- **Fees:** Range from ₹12 Lakhs to ₹25 Lakhs per year.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is MAMC better than VMMC?**
They are practically equal. MAMC has a slightly higher legacy brand value, while VMMC is preferred by some for its integrated IP University PG quota and modern hospital block.

**Q2. What is the Delhi Domicile benefit for Medical?**
If you passed Class 11 and 12 from a school located in Delhi, you are eligible for the **85% Delhi State Quota**, which significantly lowers the NEET cutoff compared to the 15% All India Quota.

**Q3. What is the fee for MBBS in Delhi Government colleges?**
Almost negligible. Fees at MAMC, VMMC, and LHMC range from ₹1,000 to ₹35,000 per year.

**Q4. Can I get a medical seat in Delhi with 550 marks?**
In government colleges, NO (General Category). However, at 550+ marks, you are in a very strong position for **Jamia Hamdard** or top-tier private medical colleges in Uttar Pradesh through the open state quota.

**Q5. When is the Delhi NEET 2026 counselling?**
Usually starts in **July/August**, conducted by the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) for central seats and Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University (GGSIPU) for state seats.
