The Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) is the premier national-level entrance exam for Law in India. It is the primary gateway for admission to 24 National Law Universities (NLUs) and several other top-tier law schools across the country. If you aspire to build a career in Corporate Law, Litigation, or the Judiciary, cracking CLAT is your first major milestone.

Here is the complete guide to the CLAT exam for 2026 admissions:

## 📊 CLAT Exam Overview

| Feature | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Exam Name** | Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) |
| **Conducting Body** | Consortium of National Law Universities (NLUs) |
| **Frequency** | Once a year (Typically held in December) |
| **Mode of Exam** | Offline (Pen and Paper based) |
| **Language** | English |

## 🎓 Eligibility Criteria

To appear for CLAT, candidates must meet the following requirements:

### For Undergraduate (UG) - 5 Year Integrated LL.B
*   **Qualification:** Passed or appearing in the 12th-grade examination from a recognized board.
*   **Minimum Marks:** 
    *   45% aggregate marks for General/OBC/PWD/NRI/PIO/OCI categories.
    *   40% aggregate marks for SC/ST categories.
*   **Age Limit:** There is no upper age limit for CLAT UG.

### For Postgraduate (PG) - LL.M
*   **Qualification:** LL.B degree or equivalent from a recognized university.
*   **Minimum Marks:**
    *   50% aggregate marks for General/OBC/PWD/NRI/PIO/OCI categories.
    *   45% aggregate marks for SC/ST categories.
*   **Age Limit:** There is no upper age limit for CLAT PG.

## 📝 CLAT Exam Pattern (UG)

The CLAT UG exam is designed to test your aptitude and reasoning skills rather than rote learning.

| Component | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Total Questions** | 120 Questions |
| **Total Duration** | 120 Minutes (2 Hours) |
| **Question Type** | Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) |
| **Marking Scheme** | +1 for Correct Answer, -0.25 for Incorrect Answer |
| **Mode** | Offline (OMR Sheet) |

## 🔍 Sectional Breakdown (CLAT UG)

The exam is divided into five key sections, each carrying a specific weightage:

1.  **English Language (22-26 questions):** Focuses on comprehension, vocabulary, and grammar.
2.  **Current Affairs including General Knowledge (28-32 questions):** Tests knowledge of national and international events, including legal developments.
3.  **Legal Reasoning (28-32 questions):** Assesses your ability to identify and apply legal principles to hypothetical situations.
4.  **Logical Reasoning (22-26 questions):** Evaluates your ability to analyze arguments, patterns, and draw inferences.
5.  **Quantitative Techniques (10-14 questions):** Tests basic mathematical operations (Class 10 level) through data interpretation.

## 🏛️ Top NLUs Participating in CLAT

Based on NIRF rankings and placement records, here are the top NLUs you should aim for:

*   **National Law School of India University (NLSIU), Bangalore** – NIRF Rank #1. The undisputed top law school in India with 100% placements.
*   **NALSAR University of Law, Hyderabad** – NIRF Rank #3. Known for excellent faculty and strong corporate law placements.
*   **The West Bengal National University of Juridical Sciences (WBNUJS), Kolkata** – One of the original top 5 NLUs with a stellar alumni network.
*   **National Law University (NLU), Jodhpur** – Pioneer NLU in Rajasthan, known for its research culture.
*   **Gujarat National Law University (GNLU), Gandhinagar** – NIRF Rank #5. Strong focus on international law and moot courts.
*   **National Law Institute University (NLIU), Bhopal** – One of the oldest NLUs with consistent placement records.

## 💡 Preparation Tips for CLAT 2026

*   **Read Regularly:** Law is all about reading. Develop a habit of reading editorials from The Hindu or The Indian Express.
*   **Master Legal Reasoning:** You don't need to know the law in-depth, but you must understand how to apply it.
*   **Practice Mock Tests:** Since there is a negative marking, speed and accuracy are critical. Take at least 25-30 full-length mocks.
*   **Stay Updated:** Follow legal news and Supreme Court judgments closely.
