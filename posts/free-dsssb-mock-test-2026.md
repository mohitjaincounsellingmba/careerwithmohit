## Breaking Down the Three-Part DSSSB Exam Structure

### Part A: General Paper (100 Questions Total)

**General Awareness, Reasoning & Quantitative Aptitude (60 Q):**
- **General Awareness:** Delhi-specific GK (Delhi government schemes, Delhi history, Delhi High Court, Aam Aadmi Party policies), national and international current affairs, basic history/polity/geography
- **Reasoning:** Series, Analogy, Coding-Decoding, Data Sufficiency, Puzzles (SSC CGL level)
- **Quantitative Aptitude:** Arithmetic (Percentage, Ratio, P&L), Algebra basics, Data Interpretation

**Hindi & English Language (40 Q):**
- **English:** Grammar (noun, pronoun, tense, prepositions), Vocabulary, Reading Comprehension (1 passage)
- **Hindi:** Vyakaran (kaal, sandhi, samas, muhavare), comprehension, error detection in Hindi sentences

### Part B: Subject Knowledge & Pedagogy (100 Questions)
This is the most important section — and the most differentiating. Content varies by the post applied for:

**For PRT (Primary Teachers):**
- Child Development & Pedagogy (30 Q) — Piaget, Vygotsky, constructivism, inclusive education
- Mathematics (20 Q) — Class 1–5 curriculum + math pedagogy
- Environmental Studies (20 Q) — Class 1–5 EVS + EVS pedagogy
- Hindi & English Pedagogy (15+15 Q) — Language teaching methods

**For TGT (Trained Graduate Teachers):**
- Subject-specific knowledge (Physics/Chemistry/Biology/Maths/Social Science/History/English/Hindi)
- Pedagogy of teaching that subject (15–20 Q)
- General pedagogy and classroom management

## Delhi Special GK — What Makes DSSSB Unique

Unlike national exams, DSSSB's GA section includes **Delhi-specific questions** that only Delhi residents typically know:

- **Delhi Government:** Current CM, Cabinet, Lt. Governor's role, Delhi's unique legislative limitation (not a full state)
- **Delhi Schemes:** Mohalla Clinics, Free Bus Travel Scheme (women), Free Electricity (subsidized), Delhi Nursery Admission Lottery system
- **Delhi Education Model:** "Happiness Curriculum" (Delhi's unique school curriculum initiative), Mission Buniyaad, Chunauti 2018
- **Delhi History:** Qutb Minar, Red Fort (capital of Mughal Empire), Lutyens' Delhi (British era), Delhi Sultanate, 1947 Partition's impact on Delhi
- **Delhi Courts:** Delhi High Court, jurisdiction, important verdicts affecting Delhi government schools

## Section-Wise Strategy for DSSSB 2026

- **General Paper (100 Q, 60 min allocated):** Attempt GK and Reasoning first (these are quicker — 40–45 minutes), then switch to Language in the last 15 minutes. Target 75+ correct.
- **Subject Knowledge (100 Q, 60 min allocated):** If you are a domain expert (e.g., a TGT Maths aspirant), this section should be your strongest. Aim for 80+ correct.
- **Pedagogy Focus:** Even subject experts often underestimate pedagogy — 20–25 Q in pedagogy require specific knowledge of teaching theories. Study B.Ed pedagogy notes carefully.

## Pro Tips for DSSSB 2026

- **NCERT is Primary Source for PRT/TGT:** Whether you're applying for Primary Teacher or TGT, NCERT Class 1–10 textbooks directly cover 80% of subject knowledge questions.
- **B.Ed Notes are Gold:** If you have a B.Ed degree, your pedagogy notes and education psychology textbooks are directly relevant — revise them systematically.
- **Delhi Current Affairs are Testable:** Follow Delhi's AAP government announcements, school improvement schemes, and court orders regarding education in Delhi.
- **Practice 200 Q in 120 Min:** This requires extremely high speed. Don't slow down in the General Paper — treat it like SSC CGL speed where you average 30 seconds on GK questions.
- **Typing Test for Clerical Roles:** If you're applying for LDC/JSA/Clerk posts, practice typing (English 35 WPM, Hindi 30 WPM) as it's mandatory after written selection.
