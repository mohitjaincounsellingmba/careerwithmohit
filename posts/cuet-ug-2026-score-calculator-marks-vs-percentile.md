## 🧮 How to Calculate CUET UG Score 2026?

Before the official results are formally announced, students usually rely on the NTA response sheet to gauge their standing. Here is a step-by-step approach to **calculate CUET score without official answer key** or using the provisional ones.

### The CUET 2026 Negative Marking Formula

To **calculate CUET UG raw score**, you must understand the official **CUET UG 2026 marking scheme** laid down by the National Testing Agency (NTA):
*   **Correct Answer:** +5 Marks
*   **Incorrect Answer:** -1 Mark (This is the **CUET 2026 negative marking formula**)
*   **Unattempted:** 0 Marks

If you want to manually calculate your raw marks, the formula is:
**(Total Correct Answers × 5) - (Total Incorrect Answers × 1) = CUET Raw Score**

### How to Use CUET Response Sheet to Calculate Marks

Once the **CUET UG answer key 2026 download** link is available on the official NTA portal, log in and download your response sheet alongside the provisional answer key. 
1. Tally your responses with the correct options.
2. Separate your counts for correct attempts, incorrect attempts, and unattempted questions.
3. Apply the marking formula for each subject individually.

## 📈 The CUET Normalization Process Explained

Because CUET is conducted over multiple dates and shifts, the difficulty level of the question papers bound to vary. To ensure a level playing field, the NTA employs a statistical process called "normalization."

### NTA Normalization Formula CUET 2026
The NTA uses the "Equipercentile Method" to determine a candidate's normalized score. In simple terms, **calculating CUET percentile from raw marks** depends not just on how many marks you scored, but *how you performed compared to all other candidates in your specific shift*.

This means a raw score of 180 out of 200 in a "hard shift" might equate to a higher percentile than a 190 in an "easy shift." This is exactly why using a sophisticated **CUET Percentile Predictor 2026** helps give you a realistic idea rather than relying solely on raw marks.
