## 🏛️ The Icons: BMCRI & St. John's

### 1. Bangalore Medical College & Research Institute (BMCRI)
Connected to the historic Victoria, Vani Vilas, and Bowring Hospitals. It is the topmost government option in Karnataka.
- **Top Choice:** For students who want an incredibly low fee structure and unmatched patient volume.

### 2. St. John’s Medical College
Ranked consistently as one of India's top 10 medical institutes. It is famous for its academic discipline, mission-driven healthcare, and excellent research output.
- **USP:** High reputation for Post-Graduate (MD/MS) success.

For a 2026 medical student, Bangalore offers niche opportunities:

## 🏗️ Karnataka NEET Counselling (KEA) 2026

The **Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA)** conducts the majority of admissions.
- **85% State Quota:** For Karnataka domicile students.
- **Open Category Seats:** Karnataka is an "Open State," meaning students from other states can apply for private medical college seats in Bangalore through KEA—these are highly competitive.

---

## 🏗️ Direct MBBS Admission in Bangalore 2026

Management quota and NRI seats in Bangalore are highly sought after.
- **Process:** Must qualify for NEET. Then register for the KEA counselling under the 'Q' or 'N' category.
- **Warning:** Bangalore has some of the highest management quota fees in India (often ₹30L - ₹50L+ per year). Always verify the bond and extra fees before signing up.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is St. John’s Bangalore better than many government colleges?**
For **Clinical Ethics and Infrastructure**, YES. St. John's is respected on par with top government institutions and has a very high alumni placement rate in global hospitals.

**Q2. Does Bangalore have any Deemed Medical universities?**
While many top colleges like BMCRI and St. John's are affiliated with RGUHS (State Health University), institutions like MS Ramaiah are now becoming part of private university umbrellas.

**Q3. What is the fee for MBBS in Karnataka Government colleges?**
For the government quota, fees are around ₹60,000 to ₹75,000 per year.

**Q4. Can a student from North India get a medical seat in Bangalore?**
Yes. You can apply for "Open Category" seats in private medical colleges through the KEA portal. However, the cutoffs for these seats are usually high (530-580+ range).

**Q5. When is the KEA Medical counselling 2026?**
Usually starts in **July** after the NEET results are announced.
