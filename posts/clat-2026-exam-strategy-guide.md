## 📝 CLAT 2026 Exam Pattern & Syllabus

The CLAT UG exam is a 2-hour test consisting of 120 multiple-choice questions. It is a **Comprehension-Based** test designed to check your reading speed and logical deduction.

### 1. English Language (20%)
- **Focus:** Reading comprehension, vocabulary, and grammar.
- **Tip:** Read editorials from *The Hindu* or *The Indian Express* daily to improve reading speed.

### 2. Current Affairs & GK (25%)
- **Focus:** International news, legal developments, awards, and sports.
- **Tip:** Focus heavily on the "Legal Current Affairs" of the last 12 months.

### 3. Legal Reasoning (25%)
- **Focus:** Applying legal principles to factual situations. You do not need "prior knowledge of law," but you need "legal logic."
- **Tip:** Solve previous year papers to understand the "Principle-Fact" application.

### 4. Logical Reasoning (20%)
- **Focus:** Syllogisms, analogies, and critical reasoning (assumptions, conclusions).
- **Tip:** Focus on **Critical Reasoning** as CLAT has moved away from analytical/puzzle-based logic.

### 5. Quantitative Techniques (10%)
- **Focus:** 10th-grade Mathematics (Data Interpretation, Ratios, Percentages).
- **Tip:** Don't skip this. This 10-12 mark section is often the decider between a Tier-1 and Tier-2 NLU.

## 🚀 12-Month CLAT 2026 Preparation Plan

- **Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Foundation**
    - Build reading speed.
    - Understand the concepts of Syllogisms and basic Arithematic.
    - Start reading a national daily newspaper.
- **Phase 2 (Months 4-7): Sectional Mastery**
    - Solve 500+ Legal Reasoning questions.
    - Deep dive into Static GK and monthly Current Affairs.
    - Take 1 Mock Test every 15 days.
- **Phase 3 (Months 8-11): Mock Drills**
    - Increase mock frequency (2 per week).
    - Analyze every mistake in the mocks.
    - Focus on time management (2 hours for 120 questions).
- **Phase 4 (Month 12): Revision**
    - Revise last 12 months of GK.
    - Focus on maintaining high accuracy.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. can I crack CLAT 2026 without coaching?**
Yes. Many toppers use self-study along with a good **Mock Test Series**. However, coaching provides a structured environment and competitive peer group.

**Q2. Is CLAT 2026 online or offline?**
Historically, CLAT is an **Offline (Pen & Paper)** exam. You mark your answers on an OMR sheet.

**Q3. What is a good score for NLSIU Bangalore?**
For the general category, a score of **100+ out of 120** is usually required to be in the top-50 rank bracket for NLSIU.

**Q4. Are there any negative marks in CLAT?**
Yes. **0.25 marks** are deducted for every wrong answer.

**Q5. Can a Commerce student clear CLAT?**
Absolutely. In fact, many CLAT toppers are from Commerce and Humanities backgrounds as they are comfortable with general studies and business logic.
