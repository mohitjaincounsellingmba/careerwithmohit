The Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) are the pinnacle of management education in India. With IIM Mumbai (formerly NITIE) joining the ranks, there are now 21 IIMs offering world-class exposure.

This guide provides a bulleted breakdown of everything you need to know for the 2026 admission cycle.

---

### The Complete List of 21 IIMs

The IIMs are generally categorized into three generations:

*   **BLACKI (Old/Top IIMs):** Ahmedabad, Bangalore, Calcutta, Lucknow, Indore, Kozhikode.
*   **New IIMs:** Shillong, Rohtak, Ranchi, Raipur, Udaipur, Trichy, Kashipur, Mumbai (converted).
*   **Baby IIMs:** Nagpur, Visakhapatnam, Amritsar, Bodh Gaya, Sambalpur, Sirmaur, Jammu.

---

### Key Courses Offered at IIMs

While the MBA is the flagship, IIMs offer diverse programs:

*   **PGP / MBA:** The core 2-year full-time program for graduates.
*   **PGP-BA / MBA-BA:** Specialized 2-year program in Business Analytics (Offered by IIM B, C, K, etc.).
*   **IPM (Integrated Programme in Management):** 5-year dual-degree (BBA+MBA) for students after Class 12 (Offered by IIM Indore, Rohtak, Ranchi, Bodh Gaya, Jammu).
*   **PGPX / EPGP:** 1-year MBA for working professionals with 5+ years of experience.
*   **Specialized PGP:**
    *   **FABM:** Food and Agri-Business Management (IIM Ahmedabad).
    *   **HRM:** Human Resource Management (IIM Indore, Ranchi).
    *   **SM:** Sustainable Management (IIM Lucknow).
    *   **Agri-Business:** (IIM Lucknow).
*   **PhD / FPM:** Doctoral programs for research and academia.

### IIM Placements 2024 - 2025 (Average Packages)

Placement trends remain strong despite global market shifts. Here are the approximate average packages:

*   **Tier 1 (IIM A, B, C):** ₹32 LPA – ₹36 LPA.
*   **Tier 2 (IIM L, K, I, Mumbai):** ₹28 LPA – ₹32 LPA.
*   **New IIMs (Shillong to Udaipur):** ₹16 LPA – ₹20 LPA.
*   **Baby IIMs (Nagpur to Jammu):** ₹12 LPA – ₹16 LPA.
*   **Highest Packages:** Often touch ₹60 LPA – ₹1.1 Crore+ (Domestic) and ₹80 LPA+ (International).

### Expected CAT 2025 Cut-offs (For 2026 Admissions)

IIMs use a two-step cut-off system: Qualifying (to be eligible) and Final Shortlist (to get an interview).

*   **Top IIMs (ABC):** 99.5+ Percentile (General), 95+ (NC-OBC), 90+ (SC/ST).
*   **IIM Lucknow, Indore, Kozhikode:** 98+ Percentile (General), 90-94 (NC-OBC).
*   **New IIMs & CAP Rounds:** 94 - 96 Percentile (General), 85+ (NC-OBC).
*   **Baby IIMs:** 90 - 94 Percentile (General), 75-80 (NC-OBC).
*   **Sectional Cut-offs:** Most IIMs require at least 75-85 percentile in each of the three sections (VARC, DILR, QA).

### The IIM Admission Process: Step-by-Step

Securing a seat involves more than just a high CAT score:

*   **Step 1: CAT Entrance Exam:** Competitive exam testing Aptitude, Reasoning, and English.
*   **Step 2: Shortlisting for Interviews:** Based on CAT score, Class 10/12 marks, Graduation percentage, and Work Experience.
*   **Step 3: Written Ability Test (WAT):** Candidates are given a topic and 15–30 minutes to write a structured essay.
*   **Step 4: Personal Interview (PI):** A final round with faculty/alumni testing your clarity, personality, and knowledge.
*   **Step 5: Composite Score Calculation:** IIMs calculate a final score using a weighted average:
    *   CAT Score: 30% - 60% weightage.
    *   Interview Performance: 20% - 40% weightage.
    *   Academics & Work-Ex: 10% - 20% weightage.
    *   Gender/Academic Diversity: 2% - 5% bonus points.

---

### Summary Tips for Aspirants

*   **Start Early:** IPM aspirants should start after Class 11; MBA aspirants ideally start CAT prep 8–10 months in advance.
*   **Focus on Profiles:** High academics and quality work experience are valued as much as CAT scores at IIM Bangalore and Ahmedabad.
*   **Practice Writing:** Don't ignore WAT; it's often the deciding factor when interview scores are tied.
