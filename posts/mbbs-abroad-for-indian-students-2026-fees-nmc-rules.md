## ⚠️ The Mandatory NMC 2026 Rules

Before you book your ticket, ensure the university follows these **NMC (National Medical Commission)** rules to ensure your degree is valid in India:
- **Duration:** The course must be of at least **54 months (4.5 years)** plus a 12-month internship.
- **Medium:** The entire course must be in **English**. Bilingual courses (3 yrs Russian + 2 yrs English) are NO LONGER VALID for registration in India.
- **License to Practice:** You must be eligible to get a license to practice in the country where you studied.
- **NEET:** You MUST be **NEET-Qualified** (minimum percentile) in the year of admission.

## 🚀 The NEXT / FMGE Reality

All foreign medical graduates (FMGs) must clear the **National Exit Test (NEXT)** to get their permanent license in India.
- **Fact:** The passing rate for FMGE/NEXT for foreign graduates is usually around 15-20%.
- **Strategy:** Choose a university that has a strong track record of FMGE-specialized coaching on campus.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. is a foreign medical degree valid in India?**
Yes, provided the university follows the **NMC FMGL Regulations 2021**. After graduating, you must clear the **NEXT-1** exam to start your internship in India.

**Q2. is it safe to study in Russia in 2026?**
While most university towns are far from the conflict zones, you must verify the latest travel advisories by the MEA (Ministry of External Affairs) and Ensure the university has stable flight routes via Dubai or Turkey.

**Q3. What is the total cost of MBBS in Uzbekistan?**
Uzbekistan is currently the cheapest. You can finish your entire degree (Tuition + Hostel + Food) in approximately **₹22 Lakhs to ₹25 Lakhs**.

**Q4. Can I practice in the USA after MBBS in Georgia?**
Yes. You must clear the **USMLE Step 1 and Step 2**. Georgian universities are often recognized by the ECFMG, which is the body that permits you to take the USMLE.

**Q5. When is the intake for MBBS Abroad 2026?**
Most universities have their primary intake in **September/October**. Some also offer a "Spring Intake" in February/March.
