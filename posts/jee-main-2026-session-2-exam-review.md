### **📊 Overall Exam Review (First Few Shifts)**
Based on expert reviews and student reactions from the early examination dates (April 2 and April 4), the overall difficulty level of the JEE Main 2026 Session 2 can be classified as **Moderate**. 

Comparing this attempt to the Session 1 (January Attempt), the paper structure and difficulty have remained largely consistent. However, students noted that specific numerical sections required more time than anticipated.

| Subject | Difficulty Level | Time Consumption |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Physics** | Easy to Moderate | Standard |
| **Chemistry** | Easy | Lowest |
| **Mathematics** | Moderate to Difficult | Very High (Lengthy) |

---

### **📚 Subject-Wise Detailed Analysis**

#### **1. Mathematics: The Deciding Factor**
As has been the trend for the past few years, the **Mathematics section was the toughest and most lengthy**. 
* **Key Topics:** Vectors & 3D Geometry, Calculus (Integration), Matrices & Determinants, and Coordinate Geometry observed maximum weightage.
* **Review:** Students found the calculations in the numerical value sections extremely time-consuming. Aspirants who managed their time well and picked the "doable" questions had a massive advantage here.

#### **2. Physics: Balanced & Formula-Based**
The Physics section provided relief to many students, being rated as **Easy to Moderate**.
* **Key Topics:** Modern Physics, Current Electricity, Kinematics, Thermodynamics, and Electromagnetism.
* **Review:** A large chunk of questions was direct formula-based. There were also a few assertion-reasoning questions that tested the conceptual clarity of the candidates.

#### **3. Chemistry: Scoring & NCERT Focused**
Chemistry continued its streak of being the **most scoring and straightforward** section of the paper.
* **Key Topics:** Inorganic Chemistry (p-block, bonding), Organic Chemistry (GOC, Biomolecules), and Physical Chemistry (Solutions, Electrochemistry).
* **Review:** Physical chemistry questions were mostly restricted to the numerical section. The inorganic and organic sections were highly NCERT-driven, rewarding students who thoroughly read the textbook.

---

### **🗣️ Student Reactions**
Most students exiting the exam centers expressed a mixed bag of emotions. 
* *"The chemistry section was a breeze and took me less than 40 minutes, but maths completely derailed my time management,"* said one aspirant. 
* *"Physics had a lot of formula-based questions. If you had revised your short notes well, scoring 70+ in physics was very achievable,"* noted another.

---

### **🎯 Good Attempts & Expected Cutoffs**
For a moderate-level paper like this, attempting around **45 to 55 questions** with high accuracy is considered a very safe zone for securing a good percentile (95+). 

To qualify for **JEE Advanced 2026**, the expected general category cutoff percentile is predicted to hover around the **91 to 93 percentile** mark, similar to the previous trends.
