As we enter the financial year 2026-27, understanding the latest **India Income Tax Slabs** is crucial for every salaried professional and business owner. With the government's continued focus on simplifying the tax structure, the **New Tax Regime** has become the default choice for most, though the **Old Tax Regime** still holds significant benefits for those with high investments.

This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of the tax brackets for FY 2026-27 to help you plan your savings effectively.

---

## 🏛️ 1. The New Tax Regime (FY 2026-27)

Designed for simplicity, the New Tax Regime offers lower tax rates but requires you to forgo most deductions (like 80C, 80D, and HRA). 
- **Standard Deduction:** Increased to **₹75,000** for salaried individuals.
- **Tax Rebate:** No tax is payable for taxable income up to **₹7,00,000** (effective income up to ₹7.75L including standard deduction).
- **Best For:** Individuals with minimal investments in insurance or house property.

---

## 🏛️ 2. The Old Tax Regime (FY 2026-27)

The Old Regime allows you to reduce your taxable income using various deductions.
- **Section 80C:** Deductions up to ₹1.5 Lakhs (LIC, PPF, ELSS, etc.).
- **Section 80D:** Medical insurance premiums.
- **HRA & LTA:** Tax-free salary components.
- **Home Loan Interest:** Deduction up to ₹2 Lakhs under Section 24(b).
- **Best For:** High-income earners with significant home loans and tax-saving investments.

---

## 🚀 3. Which One Should You Choose?

The "Switch-over point" generally lies around **₹3.75 Lakhs to ₹4.25 Lakhs** of total deductions.
- If your total eligible deductions are **above ₹4 Lakhs**, the **Old Regime** may result in lower tax.
- If your deductions are **below ₹3 Lakhs**, the **New Regime** is almost always better.

---

## 🏗️ 4. Surcharge & Health and Education Cess

- **Cess:** A uniform **4% Health and Education Cess** is applied to the total tax calculated in both regimes.
- **Surcharge:** Higher surcharges apply for individuals with income exceeding ₹50 Lakhs, capped at **25%** under the New Regime.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. can I switch between New and Old regimes every year?**
Salaried individuals can choose and switch between the regimes every year at the time of filing their ITR. However, individuals with Business/Professional income can only make this switch **once in a lifetime**.

**Q2. is standard deduction available in the New Tax Regime?**
Yes. For FY 2026-27, a standard deduction of **₹75,000** is available for salted employees and pensioners in the New Tax Regime.

**Q3. What happens if I forget to choose a regime?**
By default, your employer and the income tax portal will process your file using the **New Tax Regime** rules. You must explicitly opt for the Old Regime if you wish to claim deductions.

**Q4. is there a tax slab for senior citizens (60-80 years)?**
Under the **Old Regime**, the basic exemption limit for senior citizens is ₹3,00,000. Under the **New Regime**, the slabs are the same for all age groups.

**Q5. When is the last date to file Income Tax for 2026?**
The deadline for individual taxpayers whose accounts don't need to be audited is **July 31, 2026**.
