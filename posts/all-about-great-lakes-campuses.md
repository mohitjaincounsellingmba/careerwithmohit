**Great Lakes Institute of Management (GLIM)** has rapidly cemented its position as one of India's top private B-schools since its inception in 2004. With two stellar campuses in Chennai and Gurgaon, Great Lakes is famous for its analytics-heavy curriculum and its pioneering 1-year fast-track management program.

If you are considering GLIM for your management education, this guide breaks down the critical differences between the two campuses, their flagship programs, fee structures, 2025 placement records, and expected entrance exam cutoffs for 2026.

---

## The Programs: PGPM vs PGDM

Before picking a campus, it's vital to understand Great Lakes' two distinct programs, as they cater to entirely different applicant profiles:

1.  **PGPM (Post Graduate Program in Management):** A 1-year, fast-track MBA equivalent designed **strictly for professionals with 2+ years of work experience**. It is incredibly rigorous and boasts excellent lateral placements.
2.  **PGDM (Post Graduate Diploma in Management):** A traditional 2-year program designed for **freshers and candidates with less than 3 years of work experience**.

---

## 1. Great Lakes, Chennai (The Flagship Campus)

The Chennai campus is the flagship institute, holding prestigious AMBA and SAQS accreditations. It is renowned for its AI and Analytics curriculum.

### Fees (2025-2026)
*   **PGDM (2-Year):** ~₹22.50 Lakhs (Including accommodation)
*   **PGPM (1-Year):** ~₹23.50 Lakhs (Including accommodation)

### Placement Highlights (Class of 2025)
Chennai campus reported stellar 100% placements for PGDM and strong 90%+ placements for PGPM.
*   **Overall Highest Package:** ₹39.30 LPA
*   **PGPM Average Package:** ₹17.80 LPA
*   **PGDM Average Package:** ₹15.00 LPA
*   **Top Recruiters:** Accenture, Deloitte, EY, Nestlé, J.P. Morgan Chase, PwC, ITC, Swiggy, ADP. Consulting made up 61% of total offers.

### Expected Cutoffs (2026 Admissions)
Great Lakes focuses heavily on profile-based evaluation (work ex, past academics, essays) rather than just exam scores, so there is no rigid hard cutoff. However, safe percentiles are:
*   **CAT:** 85 - 90+ Percentile
*   **XAT:** 80 - 90 Percentile
*   **GMAT:** 600+ Score
*   **CMAT:** 95+ Percentile (mostly for PGDM)

**The Verdict:** GLIM Chennai's 1-year PGPM is one of the best in the country for lateral hiring. If you have 2-4 years of IT/Tech experience and want to pivot into Consulting or Product Management, this is an exceptional choice.

---

## 2. Great Lakes, Gurgaon (Delhi-NCR)

Established in 2011, the Gurgaon campus leverages its strategic location in the Delhi-NCR corporate hub to offer excellent industry immersion and networking.

### Fees (2025-2026)
*   **PGDM (2-Year):** ~₹19.82 Lakhs 
*   **PGPM (1-Year):** ~₹22.50 Lakhs 
*   **Online PGDM:** ₹3.70 Lakhs (₹6.00 Lakhs with campus immersion)

### Placement Highlights (Class of 2025)
*   **PGDM Highest Package:** ₹22.70 LPA
*   **PGDM Average Package:** ₹11.80 LPA
*   **PGPM Highest Package:** ₹22.10 LPA
*   **PGPM Average Package:** ₹15.10 LPA
*   **Top Recruiters:** Genpact, Cognizant, Deloitte, KPMG, Infosys, HCL Technologies.

### Expected Cutoffs (2026 Admissions)
Cutoffs for Gurgaon are slightly more relaxed than Chennai but are climbing rapidly due to the campus's rising reputation.
*   **CAT:** 80 - 85+ Percentile
*   **XAT:** 80 Percentile
*   **GMAT:** 600+ Score

**The Verdict:** An incredibly strong alternative to the Chennai campus. The Gurgaon campus provides slightly lower average packages but compensates with a slightly lower fee (for PGDM) and massive localized opportunities in the NCR region's consulting and tech sectors.

## Final Thoughts
Both Great Lakes campuses offer phenomenal ROI, particularly for their 1-year PGPM program. If you are a fresher, the 2-year PGDM at Chennai competes fiercely with top tier-2 IIMs and elite private colleges like Symbiosis and NMIMS. 

Ensure your application essays are top-notch, as Great Lakes places significant weight on your "Analytics potential" and "Learning agility" during the interview stages!
