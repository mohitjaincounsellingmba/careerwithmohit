For MBA aspirants from Bihar, the **District Registration and Counseling Centre (DRCC)**—under the **Bihar Student Credit Card (MNSSBY) Scheme**—is a lifeline. It provides collateral-free education loans up to ₹4 Lakhs for higher studies, making premier management education accessible to thousands of students.

However, not every private B-school is eligible for this scheme. The government maintains a strict list of approved institutions based on accreditations like NAAC ('A' grade or above), NBA, and NIRF rankings.

If you are planning to pursue an MBA or PGDM using the DRCC scheme, here is a comprehensive, region-wise list of the top DRCC-approved private B-schools across India's major educational hubs.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about DRCC MBA Admissions

### 1. What is the maximum loan amount under the DRCC Scheme?
The Bihar Student Credit Card scheme provides an education loan of up to **₹4,00,000 (Four Lakhs)**. If your MBA program's fees exceed this amount, you will need to fund the remainder out of pocket or apply for a supplementary bank loan.

### 2. Can I get a DRCC loan for a PGDM program?
Yes, absolutely. PGDM programs approved by AICTE at institutes that hold necessary accreditations (like NBA or an 'A' grade from NAAC) are eligible for DRCC funding. Many colleges on our list, like BIMTECH and Jaipuria, offer PGDM programs.

### 3. What documents are required by the college for DRCC processing?
You must obtain a "Bonafide Certificate" and a detailed "Fee Structure" document (signed and stamped by the college authorities) explicitly mentioning year-wise tuition, hostel, and mess charges. As noted earlier, colleges in Maharashtra often face stricter scrutiny, so ensure their fee breakdown complies perfectly with DRCC formats.

### 4. Are there any hidden charges from the college for accepting DRCC?
No reputable, top-tier B-school will charge extra for processing DRCC applications. However, since the government disburses the loan directly to the college in installments, some colleges might require you to pay the first semester's admission fee upfront to block your seat, which is later adjusted or refunded once the DRCC funds arrive. 

*Disclaimer: The DRCC eligibility of private institutions is subject to dynamic government reviews based on NAAC/NBA renewals. Always verify the college's current DRCC/MNSSBY portal status with their admission office before paying your booking amount.*
