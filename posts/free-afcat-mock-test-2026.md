## Section-Wise Strategy for AFCAT

### English (30 Q, 90 Marks)
Focus on:
- **Comprehension:** One or two RC passages on scientific or social topics
- **Error Detection:** Grammatical errors (tense, subject-verb agreement, prepositions)
- **Vocabulary:** Synonyms, antonyms, idioms and phrases — particularly unusual words
- **Sentence Completion & Para Jumbles**

### General Knowledge (25 Q, 75 Marks)
IAF-specific topics to master:
- **Defence:** IAF history, aircraft types, operation names (Operation Safed Sagar, Operation Ganga, Operation Dost)
- **Sports:** Indian Olympic and CWG medallists, international sports news
- **History:** Modern Indian history, freedom movement, key personalities
- **Geography:** Physical features, capitals, currencies, space science
- **Current Affairs:** Last 6 months — national and international

### Numerical Ability (20 Q, 60 Marks)
- Arithmetic: Percentage, Ratio, Profit-Loss, Time-Work, Speed-Distance
- Basic Algebra: Linear equations
- Data Interpretation: Simple tables and bar graphs
- Target: 16+ correct (Class 10 level is sufficient)

### Reasoning & Military Aptitude (25 Q, 75 Marks)
- **Spatial Ability:** Rotating 3D figures, mirror images, paper folding
- **Verbal Reasoning:** Analogies, classifications, series completion
- **Military Symbols & Patterns:** Military decision scenarios, sequencing
- **Number/Letter Series:** Same as standard competitive exams

## AFCAT 2 CDSE (EKT) — Technical Branch Candidates

If you are applying for the **Technical Branch**, you must also take the **Engineering Knowledge Test (EKT)** — a 30-minute, 50-question paper testing your engineering fundamentals (Aeronautical, Electronics/Mechanical). The EKT is separate from AFCAT and has no negative marking.

## AFSB — The Final Frontier

Like all defence services, AFCAT is followed by **Air Force Selection Board (AFSB)** testing:
- **Intelligence Tests** (Verbal + Non-Verbal Reasoning Battery)
- **Pilot Aptitude Battery Test** (PABT — for Flying Branch only, cannot be retaken if failed)
- **Group Psychological Tests** (WAT, TAT, SRT, SDT)
- **Group Tasks & GD**
- **Personal Interview with Air Marshal / Wing Commander**

## Pro Tips for AFCAT 2026

- **Spatial Reasoning is Unique:** Spend 30 minutes weekly on spatial/3D figure rotation practice — it's not tested in SSC/banking exams and requires dedicated practice.
- **IAF Current Affairs are Testable:** Check IAF official website and PIB Defence for IAF-specific news (new squadron inductions, bilateral exercises, airshows).
- **Flying Branch is Hyper-Competitive:** If you dream of flying fighters, prepare exceptionally well — and also solve previous year PABT-style puzzles.
- **Apply for Both AFCAT 1 & 2:** Two exams per year gives you two chances. Apply consistently until you crack it.
