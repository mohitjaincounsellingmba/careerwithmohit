## 🛠️ What is B.Tech Lateral Entry (DSE)?
Lateral entry allows students to skip the first year of the engineering program and enter directly into the **second year (3rd semester)**. In Maharashtra, this is centrally managed by the **State Common Entrance Test (CET) Cell** through the Centralized Admission Process (CAP).

### Key Highlights for 2026:
- **No Entrance Exam:** Unlike first-year admissions, DSE is purely based on the **aggregate percentage** of your Diploma or B.Sc. degree.
- **Seat Matrix:** Colleges reserve **10% of their sanctioned intake** for DSE students, plus any seats that remained vacant in the first year.

---

## 📋 Eligibility Criteria (2026)
To be eligible for the DSE CAP rounds in Maharashtra, candidates must fulfill the following:

1. **Diploma Holders:** 
   - Passed a Diploma in Engineering and Technology with at least **45% marks** (40% for Reserved categories/EWS/PwD belonging to Maharashtra State).
2. **B.Sc. Graduates:**
   - Passed B.Sc. from a UGC-recognized university with at least **45% marks** (40% for Reserved categories).
   - Must have passed **Mathematics** as a subject in 12th (HSC).
   - *Note: B.Sc. students are considered only after Diploma candidates for certain seats.*
3. **Nationality:** Must be an Indian National.

## 🚀 The DSE Admission Process (CAP)
The admission process is entirely online and follows these stages:

1. **Online Registration:** Register on the official [CET Cell Maharashtra](https://mahacet.org) portal.
2. **Document Verification (E-Scrutiny):** Upload your marksheets, leaving certificate, and category documents for online verification.
3. **Merit List Declaration:** The CET cell releases a provisional merit list, followed by a final merit list after resolving grievances.
4. **Option Form Filling:** This is the most critical step. You must list your college and branch preferences. **Pro Tip:** Always list 20-30 colleges to ensure you get a seat in competitive branches.
5. **CAP Rounds:** Seats are allotted in 3 main rounds based on your merit and preferences.
6. **Reporting:** Once allotted, accept the seat and report to the college to confirm your admission.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is MHT-CET required for Lateral Entry?**
No. Direct Second Year (DSE) admissions are based on your Diploma or B.Sc. marks, not MHT-CET scores.

**Q2. Can I change my branch during lateral entry?**
Yes, but it depends on the eligibility rules set by the DTE. Generally, you can move from core branches (e.g., Mechanical) to IT/CS, but it is highly competitive.

**Q3. When do DSE 2026 applications start?**
Typically, the registration starts in **June or July**, shortly after the final year Diploma results are announced.

**Q4. What is the fee structure for DSE?**
In government colleges like COEP, it is around ₹80k–₹1L per year. In private colleges like VIT or PICT, it ranges from ₹1.5L to ₹2.5L per year.
