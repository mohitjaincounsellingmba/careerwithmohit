The **JEE Main 2026 (Session 2)** examinations for April 4, 2026, have been successfully conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA). As thousands of aspirants appeared for the exam across India, early reports from students and experts suggest a **moderate overall difficulty level** for both Shift 1 and Shift 2.

In this post, we provide a comprehensive breakdown of the **JEE Main 4 April 2026 exam analysis**, memory-based answer keys, and subject-wise difficulty highlights to help you estimate your scores.

---

### **📊 Shift 1 Analysis (Morning Session)**
**Difficulty Level:** Moderate

*   **Mathematics**: Students reported this section as the most challenging. While the questions were not impossible, they were **extremely lengthy** and calculation-heavy. Key topics included Vectors & 3D Geometry, Integration, Matrices, and Coordinate Geometry.
*   **Physics**: Rated as **Moderate**. Most questions were formula-based, similar to previous years' patterns. A few conceptual questions from Modern Physics and Electromagnetism required deeper thinking.
*   **Chemistry**: The **easiest and most scoring** section. The paper was heavily based on **NCERT**, with a good mix of Organic (GOC, Biomolecules) and Inorganic (D & P block) chemistry.

### **📊 Shift 2 Analysis (Evening Session)**
**Difficulty Level:** Moderate to Difficult

*   **Mathematics**: Remained the toughest section. Many students found the Algebra and Calculus questions quite tricky and time-consuming.
*   **Physics**: Moderate. Topics like Mechanics and Kinematics had a significant presence.
*   **Chemistry**: Again, the easiest section. Straightforward questions from Physical Chemistry (Thermodynamics, Kinetics) and Inorganic Chemistry were seen.
