One of the most frequent dilemmas for law aspirants in India is: **"Should I join a 5-year integrated law course right after Class 12, or wait until I graduate and do a 3-year LLB?"**

While both degrees make you an advocate, the journey, the curriculum, and the initial job market reception are quite different. In this 2026 guide, we break down the **5-year vs 3-year LLB** comparison to help you make a data-backed choice.

---

## 📊 Quick Comparison: 5-Year Integrated vs 3-Year LLB

| Feature | 5-Year Integrated LLB | 3-Year LLB |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | 5 Years | 3 Years |
| **Eligibility** | 10+2 (Any Stream) | Graduation (Any Stream) |
| **Qualification** | Dual Degree (BA/BBA/B.Com + LLB) | Professional Degree (LLB) |
| **Core Audience** | High School Students | Graduates / Career Changers |
| **Syllabus Focus** | Integrated Social Science + Law | Pure Core Law Subjects |
| **Top Entrance** | CLAT, AILET, SLAT, LSAT | CUET PG, MAH CET Law, LSAT |
| **Career Goal** | Corporate Law / Global Firms | Litigation / Judiciary / Civil Services |

---

## 🏛️ 1. The Curriculum: Integration vs. Core Focus

### 5-Year Integrated LLB (e.g., BA LLB, BBA LLB)
- **The Blend:** In the first 2-3 years, you study subjects from your chosen stream (e.g., Political Science, Sociology, Finance) along with introductory Law subjects.
- **The Outcome:** You graduate with a dual degree. It provides a foundational understanding of how Law interacts with society or business.
- **Why Choose It:** If you wanted to be a lawyer since high school, this saves you **one academic year** (5 years vs 6 years for graduation+LLB).

### 3-Year LLB (Bachelor of Law)
- **The Focus:** There are no social science subjects. You dive straight into the core of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), Contracts, Corporate Law, and Torts from Day 1.
- **The Outcome:** Ideal for those who have already completed a degree in B.Tech, B.A., or B.Com and decided to add a legal dimension to their profile.
- **Why Choose It:** It’s the preferred route for career changers and those aiming for the **Judiciary** after gaining a different perspective in their first degree.

---

## 🚀 2. The Placement & Salary Reality

### Corporate Law Firms (The 5-Year Edge)
Tier-1 Law firms (like Khaitan & Co or SAM) heavily prefer graduates from **integrated 5-year courses** at Top NLUs and premium private colleges like Jindal or Symbiosis. 
- **Reason:** By the time you graduate, you have spent 5 years immersed in the legal culture through 10+ internships.

### Litigation & Judiciary (The 3-Year Edge)
Independent practice (Litigation) and Judicial Services often see incredible talent from **3-year LLB programs** at legacy institutes like **Delhi University (Faculty of Law)** or **GLC Mumbai**.
- **Reason:** Having a prior degree in Arts or Sciences often provides a more mature perspective for arguing complex cases in court.

---

## 🏗️ 3. Who Should Choose What?

**Choose 5-Year Integrated LLB if:**
- You are 100% sure about a career in Law after Class 12.
- You want to enter the **Corporate Sector** or "Big Law" firms.
- You want the benefit of an integrated curriculum that saves one year.
- You are ready for the rigor of CLAT/AILET preparation.

**Choose 3-Year LLB if:**
- You decided to join Law after completing another degree (e.g., B.Tech or B.Com).
- Your primary goal is **Litigation, Academic Research, or the Judiciary**.
- You want to focus purely on Law subjects without "distractions" like History or Management.
- You want a degree that builds on your existing specialization (e.g., Engineering + Law for IP Law).

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is 5-year LLB harder than 3-year LLB?**
Not necessarily. The 5-year course is a marathon that requires consistent academic and internship effort for half a decade. The 3-year course is a sprint that is very intensive since you cover all core law subjects in just 6 semesters.

**Q2. Are both degrees valid for practice in Indian courts?**
YES. Both are recognized by the **Bar Council of India (BCI)**. Once you clear the AIBE (All India Bar Examination), you can practice in any court in India.

**Q3. Can a 3-year LLB student get a top corporate job?**
Yes, especially if the degree is from a brand like **DU Faculty of Law** or **GLC Mumbai**. However, they might need to work harder on their internship portfolio to match the 5-year graduates.

**Q4. Does 12th Marks matter for 5-Year LLB?**
In private colleges, yes (usually 45-50% minimum). In NLUs, your rank in CLAT matters almost exclusively, provided you meet the basic eligibility.

**Q5. Can I switch from a 5-year to a 3-year course?**
No. They are separate programs. You cannot "exit" a 5-year course with just a BA/BBA degree halfway through in most Indian universities.
