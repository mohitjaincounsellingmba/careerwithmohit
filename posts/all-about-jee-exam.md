The **Joint Entrance Examination (JEE)** is the flagship engineering entrance exam in India, serving as the gateway to the world-renowned Indian Institutes of Management (IITs), National Institutes of Technology (NITs), and other premier engineering institutions like IIITs and GFTIs.

If your dream is to pursue B.Tech from a top-tier Indian engineering college, JEE is the ultimate challenge.

Here is a comprehensive guide to JEE 2026 for engineering aspirants:

## Exam Structure: Main vs. Advanced

The JEE is conducted in two distinct stages:

1. **JEE Main:** The first stage, conducted twice a year (usually in January and April). It is the qualifying criteria for JEE Advanced and the primary entrance for admission to NITs, IIITs, and many state government colleges.
2. **JEE Advanced:** Only the top ~2.5 lakh qualifiers of JEE Main are eligible to sit for JEE Advanced, which is the sole gateway to the prestigious **Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs)**.

## JEE Main 2026: Overview

- **Conducting Body:** National Testing Agency (NTA)
- **Mode of Exam:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
- **Subjects:** Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics
- **Total Questions:** 90 Questions (30 per subject)
  - *Candidates need to attempt only 75 questions (25 per subject).*
- **Total Marks:** 300 Marks
- **Marking Scheme:**
  - +4 for every correct answer
  - -1 for every incorrect MCQ
  - 0 for unattempted or incorrect Numerical value questions (check latest NTA guidelines for updates on negative marking for numericals).

## JEE Advanced 2026: Overview

- **Conducting Body:** One of the seven zonal IITs on rotation.
- **Mode of Exam:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
- **Exam Pattern:** Two compulsory papers (Paper 1 & Paper 2) of 3 hours each. Both papers happen on the same day.
- **Questions:** A mix of MCQs, Multiple Correct Option MCQs, Numerical value, and Matching list type questions.

## Top Engineering Colleges Accepting JEE Scores

1. **IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, IIT Kanpur** (Through JEE Advanced)
2. **NIT Trichy, NIT Surathkal, NIT Warangal** (Through JEE Main)
3. **IIIT Hyderabad, IIIT Bangalore, IIIT Delhi** (Through JEE Main)
4. **BITS Pilani, Goa, Hyderabad** (Note: BITS has its own exam, BITSAT, but some categories use JEE scores for direct admission).
5. **DTU & NSUT, Delhi** (Through JEE Main & JAC Delhi Counselling)

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. When will JEE Main 2026 be held?
JEE Main 2026 Session 1 is expected in **late January 2026**, and Session 2 is expected in **early April 2026**.

### 2. Can I get an IIT with JEE Main score?
No, you must qualify for JEE Advanced to be eligible for admission to any **Indian Institute of Technology (IIT)**.

### 3. What is the 75% rule in JEE?
Candidates must usually secure at least **75% marks** in their Class 12 Board exams (65% for SC/ST) or be in the top 20 percentile of their respective boards to be eligible for NIT/IIT admissions.

### 4. How many attempts are allowed for JEE Advanced?
Candidates can attempt JEE Advanced a maximum of **two times in two consecutive years**.
