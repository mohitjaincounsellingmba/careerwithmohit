The State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, is all set to conduct the second session of the **MAH MBA CET 2026** on **May 9, 2026**. This session is primarily for candidates who were either allotted this later date or were eligible for a re-examination.

With only 3 days left for the exam, here is everything you need to know about your hall ticket and the final checklist for the exam day.

## 1. MAH MBA CET May 9 Session Admit Card
The admit card for the May 9 session was officially released on **May 2, 2026**. If you haven't downloaded it yet, follow these steps:

1.  Visit the official website: **[cetcell.mahacet.org](https://cetcell.mahacet.org)**.
2.  Go to the 'Candidate Registration A.Y 2026-27' portal.
3.  Click on the link for **MAH-MBA/MMS CET 2026 Admit Card**.
4.  Log in using your Application Number and Date of Birth.
5.  Download the PDF and take a color printout.

> **Important:** Your admit card contains your specific venue, reporting time, and shift details. Double-check these immediately!

## 2. Exam Day Guidelines & Mandatory Documents
To ensure a smooth entry into the examination hall, carry the following:
- **Printed Admit Card:** With a recent photograph pasted on it (same as the one uploaded).
- **Valid Photo ID:** Original Aadhar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, or Passport.
- **Ballpoint Pen:** Blue or black for rough work.

**Strict Prohibitions:** Mobile phones, calculators, digital watches, and any electronic gadgets are strictly banned.

## 3. MAH MBA CET 2026 Result Date
A common question among aspirants is when the results will be declared. Since the exams are being conducted in two major phases (April and May), the **MAH MBA CET 2026 results are expected to be declared together in the first week of June 2026**.

Once the results are out, the **Centralized Admission Process (CAP)** will begin for admission into top colleges like JBIMS, SIMSREE, and PUMBA.

## 4. Last-Minute Tips for May 9 Session
If you are appearing for the May 9 session, keep these points in mind:
- **Analysis of April Session:** Review the memory-based questions and difficulty level of the April session. Usually, the difficulty level remains consistent across sessions.
- **Focus on Logic:** The Logical and Abstract Reasoning sections carry the maximum weightage. Ensure you have a strategy to solve these quickly.
- **No Negative Marking:** Remember there is no negative marking in MHCET. Try to attempt all questions, but don't spend too much time on a single difficult problem.
