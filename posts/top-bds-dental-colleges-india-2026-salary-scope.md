## 🏛️ The Dental Giants

### 1. Maulana Azad Institute of Dental Sciences (MAIDS), Delhi
The undisputed #1 dental college in India. Being a central institute, it offers the most advanced labs and a patient load that allows students to see thousands of varied clinical cases.
- **Fees:** Very low (subsidized).
- **Selection:** AIR rank for BDS usually needs to be in the Top 5,000 - 10,000.

### 2. Manipal College of Dental Sciences (MCODS)
Ranked as the #1 private dental college for decades. It is known for its international-standard infrastructure and high success rate in PG (MDS) entrances.

## 🚀 Career Scope & Salary in 2026

### 1. The Corporate Shift
In 2026, most BDS graduates join dental chains (like Clove Dental) or corporate hospital departments. Starting salaries have stabilized around **₹3.5 - 6.0 LPA**, with rapid scaling once you specialize (MDS).

### 2. Cosmetic & Implantology
The highest-earning sector for dentists today is Aesthetic Dentistry and Dental Implants. A BDS degree is just the start; pursuing certification in these niche areas can lead to a private practice earning ₹20L+ per year.

## 🏗️ Admission Process 2026

- **Entrance:** NEET-UG is mandatory for all BDS seats in India.
- **Counselling:** Handled by **MCC (Medical Counselling Committee)** for Central/Deemed seats and State Dental authorities for state government/private seats.
- **Seat Availability:** BDS seats are much easier to get than MBBS. You can often secure a decent private dental seat with 250-350 marks in NEET.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. is BDS better than BAMS?**
BDS is a **Surgical degree**, while BAMS is a **General Medicine degree**. If you like working with your hands and performing minor surgical procedures, BDS is better. If you prefer long-term healing and holistic medicine, BAMS is the way.

**Q2. What is the fee for BDS in private colleges?**
Private BDS fees range from ₹3 Lakhs to ₹6 Lakhs per year. Some lower-tier colleges in UP and Rajasthan offer seats at ₹2.5 Lakhs per year to fill vacancies.

**Q3. can I do MBBS after BDS?**
No. They are separate degrees. You would have to appear for NEET again and start MBBS from Year 1. However, many BDS graduates pursue an **MDS** followed by a bridge course into specialized surgery (where applicable).

**Q4. is there a bridge course for BDS to MBBS 2026?**
While discussed by the government, as of early 2026, there is no official national bridge course that converts a BDS doctor into an MBBS doctor.

**Q5. What is the salary of a Dentist in the Army?**
Dental officers in the **Army Dental Corps** are commissioned officers with salaries starting at ₹90,000+ per month, on par with other medical officers.
