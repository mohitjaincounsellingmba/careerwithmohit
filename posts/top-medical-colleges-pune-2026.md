## 🏛️ The Pune Icons: AFMC & BJMC

### 1. Armed Forces Medical College (AFMC)
India's premier defence medical college. AFMC graduates serve as commissioned officers in the Indian Armed Forces.
- **Selection:** High NEET score is just the first step. You must clear the **ToELR (English & Logic)**, a Psychological Assessment, and a strict Medical/Physical interview.

### 2. B.J. Medical College (BJMC) & Sassoon Hospital
Connected to the massive Sassoon General Hospital, BJMC is the topmost government option in Pune. It is known for its high-load trauma and infectious disease wards.
- **USP:** Incredible ROI and a very high alumni presence in top US and UK hospitals.

## 🚀 The Pune Advantage (Academics & Climate)

For a 2026 medical student, Pune offers:
- **Academic Environment:** As a student hub, Pune has hundreds of libraries and coaching centers that specifically help with high-end NEET-PG and USMLE preparation.
- **Deemed Prowess:** Pune has the highest concentration of "A+ Grade" Deemed medical universities (Bharti Vidyapeeth, Symbiosis, DY Patil) in Western India.
- **Climate:** The stable weather of Pune is significantly more conducive to the long, intensive 5.5-year MBBS grind compared to the humidity of Mumbai or the heat of Delhi.

## 🏗️ Maharashtra NEET Counselling 2026

The **State Common Entrance Test Cell** Maharashtra conducts the admissions for BJMC and other private colleges like Kashibai Navale.
- **85% State Quota:** Available only to Maharashtra domicile students.
- **Symbiosis Advantage:** Symbiosis Medical College for Women (Deemed) is one of India's few medical colleges offering specialized scholarships for meritorious female students—always check their 2026 portal.

---

## 🏗️ Direct MBBS Admission in Pune 2026

Direct admission in Pune is legally available through the "Deemed University" and "Institutional Quota" seats in colleges like Bharti Vidyapeeth and DY Patil.
- **Process:** You MUST qualify for NEET. Register under the Deemed category on the MCC website or State CET cell.
- **Fees:** Total first-year costs often range from ₹22 Lakhs to ₹32 Lakhs.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. can I get a seat in BJMC Pune with 600 marks?**
For the General category, it is very difficult. Most years, BJMC closes above **650-660**. However, with 600 marks, you can target private medical colleges in Pune like Smt. Kashibai Navale.

**Q2. is there a service bond in AFMC?**
Yes. Graduates from AFMC are committed to serving in the **Armed Forces Medical Services** for a specified period. If you wish to exit, you must pay a significant "Bond-Break" amount.

**Q3. What is the fee for MBBS in Bharti Vidyapeeth Pune?**
Bharti Vidyapeeth (Deemed) has an annual tuition fee of around ₹20 Lakhs to ₹22 Lakhs, which is consistent for the 5-year duration.

**Q4. Can a student from Karnataka apply for Pune medical seats?**
You can apply for **Deemed University seats** (AFMC, BVP, DY Patil) through MCC. For government seats (BJMC), you must be a Maharashtra domicile.

**Q5. When is the Pune Medical admission 2026 starting?**
Counselling registrations typically open in **July** after the NEET results.
