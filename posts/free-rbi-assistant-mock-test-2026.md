## Why RBI Assistant is More Competitive Than SBI/IBPS Clerk

- **Fewer Vacancies:** RBI Assistant has significantly fewer posts (typically 300–900) compared to thousands of IBPS/SBI Clerk vacancies.
- **Higher Cutoffs:** The cutoff for RBI Assistant Prelims is typically 85–92 for General category — among the highest in clerical-level banking exams.
- **Prestige Factor:** More aspirants from strong academic backgrounds compete for RBI because of the association with India's central bank.
- **Language Proficiency Test (LPT):** Selected candidates must pass an LPT in the regional language of their RBI office. This is unique to RBI Assistant.

## Section-Wise Strategy for RBI Assistant Prelims

- **English (30 Q, 20 min):** RBI English is close to SBI Clerk in difficulty. Reading Comprehension passages are on financial/economic topics. Grammar-based questions dominate. Aim for 26+ correct.
- **Quantitative (35 Q, 20 min):** Heavy on Simplification/Approximation and DI sets. Also expect Number Series and Arithmetic Word Problems. Target 28+ attempts.
- **Reasoning (35 Q, 20 min):** Multi-variable puzzles, Seating Arrangements, Syllogism, and Inequalities. The puzzle difficulty is between IBPS Clerk and IBPS PO. Target 28+ easy questions first.

After Prelims, Mains tests candidates across 5 sections:
