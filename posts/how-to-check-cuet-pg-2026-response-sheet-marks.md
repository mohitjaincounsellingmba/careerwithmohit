Knowing how to check your response sheet is the first step in calculating your raw score and predicting your chances for top Central Universities like DU, BHU, and JNU. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process step-by-step.

### **⏱️ CUET PG 2026 Response Sheet: Official Timeline**

Typically, the National Testing Agency (NTA) releases the response sheets within **7 to 10 days** after the last exam date.

*   **Final Exam Date**: March 27, 2026
*   **Expected Response Sheet Date**: April 4 – April 7, 2026
*   **Final Answer Key Release**: Late April 2026
*   **CUET PG 2026 Result Expected**: Early May 2026 ([Check Result Timeline](/blog/cuet-pg-2026-result-expected-date))

---

### **🔍 Step-by-Step Guide: How to Check CUET PG 2026 Response Sheet**

Follow these instructions to download your personalized response sheet from the NTA portal:

1.  **Visit the Official Portal**: Go to [exams.nta.ac.in/CUET-PG/](https://exams.nta.ac.in/CUET-PG/).
2.  **Login to Your Dashboard**: Click on the 'CUET PG 2026 Login' button. You will need your **Application Number** and **Date of Birth** (or Password).
3.  **Locate the Link**: After logging in, look for the tab titled **"View/Challenge Answer Key"** or **"Download Response Sheet."**
4.  **View & Download**: Your response sheet will show the question ID, the choice you selected, and the correct option (if released). Save it as a PDF immediately!
5.  **Cross-Verify**: Use the provisional answer key to match your responses and calculate your raw marks.

---

### **📝 How to Calculate Your CUET PG 2026 Score?**

The CUET PG 2026 follows a specific marking scheme. Use this formula to get your raw score:

*   **Correct Response**: +4 Marks
*   **Incorrect Response**: -1 Mark
*   **Unattempted**: 0 Marks

**Formula:**
`Total Score = (Number of Correct Answers × 4) - (Number of Incorrect Answers × 1)`

### **🚩 Raising Objections (Answer Key Challenge)**

If you find that an answer marked as "correct" by NTA is actually wrong, you have the right to challenge it.
*   **Fee**: ₹200 per question (Non-refundable).
*   **Requirement**: You must upload a valid supporting document (standard textbook reference or research paper) to justify your claim.
