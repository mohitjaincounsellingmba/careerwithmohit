## JEE Main 2026 Rank Predictor — Key Inputs

Your JoSAA college allotment depends on:

1. **JEE Main Rank (CRL)** — All-India rank across all categories
2. **Category** — General / OBC-NCL / EWS / SC / ST
3. **Home State** — Home state and other state quota seats
4. **Gender** — Supernumerary seats for female candidates (5% extra at NITs)
5. **Branch Preference** — CSE gets the highest cutoffs at every college

## FAQs — JEE Main 2026 College Predictor

**Q1. Which college can I get with a JEE Main rank of 10,000?**
With CRL 10,000, you can realistically target NITs like MNNIT Allahabad, NIT Bhopal, and NIT Jalandhar for CSE. For ECE and Mechanical, you can target NIT Trichy, NIT Warangal cutoffs.

**Q2. Can I get into NIT with 80 percentile in JEE Main?**
An 80 percentile in JEE Main typically translates to a rank of 2–2.5 Lakhs. This may not be sufficient for most NITs in Open category, but OBC/SC/ST candidates may qualify for lower-ranked NITs.

**Q3. Is 95 percentile enough for NIT CSE?**
A 95 percentile typically gives a rank of around 50,000. This is generally not enough for NIT CSE in the general category, but it may qualify for NIT ECE/Mechanical at lower-ranked NITs or IIIT CSE at state IIITs.

**Q4. What happens if I don't get a seat in JoSAA?**
You can explore seats through state counselling (UPTAC, REAP, ACPC, TNEA etc.), and direct/management quota admissions at private colleges. A good rank in private exams like BITSAT, VITEEE, MU OET is also an option.

**Q5. How many rounds are there in JoSAA 2026?**
JoSAA typically conducts 6 rounds of seat allotment. Seats move significantly between rounds — don't withdraw early if you're waiting for upgrades.
