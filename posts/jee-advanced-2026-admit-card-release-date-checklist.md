The wait for the most prestigious engineering entrance exam in India is almost over. **IIT Roorkee**, the organizing body for **JEE Advanced 2026**, has confirmed that the admit cards will be available for download starting **May 11, 2026**.

The examination is scheduled to take place on **Sunday, May 17, 2026**, in two mandatory shifts (Paper 1 and Paper 2). Here is your final roadmap and checklist for the big day.

## 1. JEE Advanced 2026 Important Dates
Mark these dates to ensure you don't miss any critical steps:

| Event | Date |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Admit Card Download Starts** | **May 11, 2026 (10:00 AM)** |
| **Admit Card Download Ends** | May 17, 2026 (02:30 PM) |
| **JEE Advanced 2026 Exam Date** | **May 17, 2026** |
| **Paper 1 Timing** | 09:00 AM to 12:00 PM |
| **Paper 2 Timing** | 02:30 PM to 05:30 PM |
| **Official Website** | [jeeadv.ac.in](https://jeeadv.ac.in) |

## 2. How to Download the Hall Ticket?
Once the link is activated on May 11, follow these steps:
1.  Visit the official portal: **[jeeadv.ac.in](https://jeeadv.ac.in)**.
2.  Click on the link "JEE (Advanced) 2026 Admit Card".
3.  Enter your **Registration Number**, **Date of Birth**, and **Mobile Number**.
4.  Download the PDF and verify all details like your name, roll number, photograph, and exam center.

> **Note:** If there are any discrepancies in your admit card, contact the Chairperson of the respective Zonal IIT immediately.

## 3. Mandatory Exam Day Checklist
You must carry the following items to the exam center:
- **Printed Admit Card:** Preferably a color printout on A4 paper.
- **Valid Original Photo ID:** (Aadhar Card, School ID, PAN Card, Passport, or Voter ID).
- **Basic Stationery:** Pens and pencils are allowed, but the exam itself is a Computer Based Test (CBT).

**Prohibited Items:** Smartwatches, mobile phones, Bluetooth devices, log tables, and any electronic gadgets are strictly forbidden.

## 4. Final Week Strategy & Tips
With the exam just around the corner, avoid starting any new topics. Focus on:
- **Mock Tests:** Take at least 2 full-length mocks in the actual exam timings (9-12 and 2:30-5:30) to build stamina.
- **Error Analysis:** Review your previous mock test errors to avoid repeating them on the final day.
