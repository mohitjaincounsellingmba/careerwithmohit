## Why SBI Clerk is Tougher Than IBPS Clerk

SBI sets a higher bar across all sections:

- **English:** SBI uses editorial-level RC passages and complex fill-in-the-blank sentence frameworks
- **Quantitative:** DI sets tend to be more data-heavy with multiple sub-questions; Simplification is faster-paced
- **Reasoning:** Multi-variable puzzles appear even at clerk level — more similar to IBPS PO reasoning than IBPS Clerk

The sectional cutoffs are also higher — with more applicants (30–40 lakh) competing for fewer vacancies.

## Section-Wise Strategy for SBI Clerk Prelims

- **English (30 Q, 20 min):** Prioritize Fill in the Blanks and Error Detection before RC. SBI RC passages are article-length — leave them for the last 5–7 minutes if you're fast at grammar.
- **Quantitative (35 Q, 20 min):** Simplification (8–10 Q) is the quickest point-scorer. Then solve 1 DI set and maximize arithmetic word problems. Skip lengthy Table DI if time is tight.
- **Reasoning (35 Q, 20 min):** Solve Inequalities and Syllogism first (10–12 Q in <5 mins). Then pick the easier of the 2 puzzle sets. Blood relations and direction questions give quick marks too.

## SBI Clerk Mains — What to Expect

After clearing Prelims, SBI Clerk Mains includes:
- **General/Financial & Banking Awareness:** 50 Questions (45 mins)
- **General English:** 40 Questions (35 mins)
- **Quantitative Aptitude & Data Interpretation:** 50 Questions (45 mins)
- **Reasoning Ability & Computer Aptitude:** 50 Questions (45 mins)

**Total: 190 Q in 2 hours 40 minutes.** No Personal Interview — pure merit-based.
