The **Rajasthan Eligibility Examination for Teacher (REET)** is the state-level teacher qualification exam conducted by the **Board of Secondary Education Rajasthan (BSER)**. Passing REET is mandatory for any candidate who wishes to be eligible for teaching positions at **Level 1 (Classes 1–5)** or **Level 2 (Classes 6–8)** in Rajasthan government schools.

With thousands of teaching vacancies in Rajasthan's vast government school system and intense competition among aspirants, a qualified REET score opens the door to a stable, respected teaching career.

## What Makes REET Unique — The Rajasthan Special Component

Unlike CTET, **REET's EVS section includes significant Rajasthan-specific content** that national TET exams don't cover:

- **Rajasthan Geography:** Districts, rivers (Chambal, Luni, Banas), deserts (Thar), hill stations (Mt Abu)
- **Rajasthan History:** Rajput kingdoms, battles of Haldighati & Khanuwa, Maharana Pratap, Prithviraj Chauhan
- **Rajasthan Polity:** State government structure, Panchayati Raj in Rajasthan
- **Rajasthan Economy:** Agriculture (bajra, jowar, guar), mining (zinc, copper), handicrafts
- **Rajasthan Culture:** Folk music (Maand, Baul), dances (Ghoomar, Kalbelia), festivals (Teej, Pushkar Fair)
- **Social Issues in Rajasthan:** Female literacy, SC/ST welfare, MNREGA in Rajasthan

This Rajasthan GK component makes REET more demanding than CTET for out-of-state candidates while giving local Rajasthan aspirants an advantage.

## Section-Wise Strategy for REET Level 1

- **CDP (30 Q):** Learn all major theorists, their stages, and key terminology. CDP is pure theory — there's no shortcut but it's entirely learnable in 3–4 weeks.
- **Language 1 (Hindi/Sanskrit):** Grammar + vocabulary + poetry/prose comprehension + language teaching methods (communicative approach, Direct Method, Grammar-Translation Method).
- **Language 2 (English):** Basic grammar, reading comprehension, and English pedagogy concepts. If English is not your Language 1, focus on familiarity with English teaching methods.
- **Mathematics (30 Q):** Class 1–5 math (numbers, shapes, fractions, measurement) + math pedagogy (problem-solving approach, math lab methods, math anxiety).
- **EVS (30 Q):** Physical science basics, plant/animal topics, family/community + Rajasthan GK. Spend equal time on both components.

## Pro Tips for REET 2026

- **Rajasthan GK is Non-Negotiable:** Attempting REET without Rajasthan-specific preparation will cost you 5–10 marks in EVS alone.
- **No Negative Marking = 150 Attempts Minimum:** Never leave a question blank. Even a random guess has a 25% chance of being right, with no downside.
- **Study NCERT Class 1–5:** The actual subject content comes from Class 1–5 NCERT textbooks. Know these inside out.
- **Apply CTET Simultaneously:** REET certifies you for Rajasthan schools; CTET gives you national recognition. Both are compatible goals.
