### The Problem with Third-Party Marketplaces

Many creators start by selling courses on aggregated marketplaces. While easy to set up, they come with significant downsides:
*   **High Revenue Share:** These platforms often take 30% to 50% of your sales.
*   **Lack of Branding:** Your students are customers of the marketplace, not your brand.
*   **Algorithm Competition:** The platform may suggest your competitor's courses right next to yours.
*   **Limited Data Access:** You don't "own" your student data, making it hard to remarket.

#### 2. Direct-to-Bank Revenue
When you use your own branded app, you cut out the middleman. Every rupee earned from your courses, mentorship programs, or digital products goes directly into your bank account.

#### 4. Built-in Community and Engagement
Move your community away from chaotic comment sections. Use integrated chat, forums, and live Q&A sessions within your app to build deeper relationships with your students.
