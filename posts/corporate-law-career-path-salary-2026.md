## 🔝 Why Corporate Law in 2026?

Unlike traditional litigation, which can take years to yield a stable income, Corporate Law offers:
1.  **High Starting Salaries:** Fresh graduates from Top 5 NLUs or Tier-1 private schools often start at ₹15L to ₹20L per annum.
2.  **Global Mobility:** Skills in international arbitration and global contracts allow you to work in London, Singapore, or Dubai hubs.
3.  **Structured Growth:** You follow a clear path from Associate → Senior Associate → Principal Associate → Partner.

## 🚀 The Path: From NLU to Boardroom

### Step 1: Choose the Right Integrated Degree
While BA LLB is great, many corporate recruiters have a slight bias towards **BBA LLB (Hons)** or **B.Com LLB (Hons)** because these students already understand business finance, taxation, and accounting.

### Step 2: Internship Strategy (The 1-3-5 Rule)
- **Year 1-2:** NGOs and Trial Courts to understand basic justice systems.
- **Year 3:** High Court Litigation and small corporate boutiques.
- **Year 4-5:** Intern solely with Tier-1 or Tier-2 Corporate Law firms to secure a **PPO (Pre-Placement Offer)**.

### Step 3: Master the Niche Specializations
Corporate law isn't just "Company Law." In 2026, the highest salaries are in:
- **TMT (Technology, Media & Telecom):** Handling data privacy and 6G regulations.
- **Insolvency and Bankruptcy (IBC):** High-stakes corporate restructuring.
- **Intellectual Property (IPR):** Protecting tech and pharmaceutical patents.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Do I need an MBA after Law for Corporate Law?**
Not necessarily. Most corporate lawyers have just a BA/BBA LLB. However, an MBA from a top school (like IIM) can help you transition into **Private Equity (PE) or Investment Banking** roles.

**Q2. is Corporate Law "harder" than Litigation?**
It involves longer hours and high-pressure deadlines (all-nighters during a merger). Litigation is more about courtroom presence and research, while Corporate Law is about drafting and strategy.

**Q3. Can a student from a private law college join Shardul Amarchand?**
YES. While they hire heavily from NLUs, they also hire the "Toppers" from premier private schools like Jindal, Symbiosis, and Christ. Your **internship performance** is the ultimate decider.

**Q4. What skills are essential for Corporate Law?**
Attention to detail (for contracts), excellent negotiation skills, and a solid understanding of commercial finance.

**Q5. Can I work in the US/UK after doing Law in India?**
Yes, but you will need to pass the Bar exam of that country (e.g., the New York Bar or the SQE in the UK) and often pursue an **LLM** from a global university.
