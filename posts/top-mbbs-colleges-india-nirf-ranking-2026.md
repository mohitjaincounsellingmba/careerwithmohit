## 🏛️ Profiles of the Medical Giants

### 1. AIIMS, New Delhi
The undisputed #1 medical institute in India and one of the finest in Asia. AIIMS is not just a college; it is a research powerhouse with a massive patient load that ensures unparalleled clinical exposure.
- **Selection:** Secured solely through **NEET-UG** national merit (requires AIR under 50-100).
- **Fees:** Almost negligible (~₹1,600 for the entire course).

### 2. Christian Medical College (CMC), Vellore
Historically ranked as the best private (minority-run) medical college in India. CMC focuses heavily on community health and ethical practice.
- **Admission Strategy:** 100% focus on NEET scores + a specialized interview/aptitude round.

### 3. Maulana Azad Medical College (MAMC), Delhi
Connected to the Lok Nayak Hospital, MAMC offers the highest patient-to-student ratio in India. If you want to be a master of "Surgery and Diagnosis" through raw clinical experience, MAMC is the goal.

## 🚀 Key Trends for Medical in 2026

If you are a 2026 NEET aspirant, watch these factors:
- **MCC Counselling Dominance:** The **Medical Counselling Committee (MCC)** handles 100% of seats in AIIMS, JIPMER, and Central Universities, plus 15% All India Quota (AIQ) in state colleges.
- **Bond Policies:** Many state government colleges (like those in Rajasthan, Haryana, and UP) have service bond requirements. Always check the bond amount before registering.
- **NEXT Exam Prep:** All MBBS batches graduating from 2026 onwards will likely have to clear the **National Exit Test (NEXT)** for medical licensing and PG admission.

---

## 🏗️ Top 3 Choice Factors for Medical Students

1.  **Patient Load:** A medical college is only as good as the hospital it is attached to. Avoid "New AIIMS" or "Tier-3 Private" colleges that have empty hospitals.
2.  **Internal PG Quota:** Some institutes (like JIPMER, AMU, BHU, and DU colleges) have a reserved quota for their own graduates in Post-Graduate (MD/MS) admissions—this is a massive long-term advantage.
3.  **Hostel & Infrastructure:** High-pressure medical study requires a stable and affordable campus life. AIIMS and AFMC offer the best residential facilities.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. can I get AIIMS Delhi with 600 marks in NEET?**
No. For the General category, AIIMS Delhi usually closes at a NEET score of **710-720**. With 600 marks, you can target mid-tier State Government medical colleges or top Private institutions like KMC Manipal.

**Q2. is AFMC Pune better than AIIMS?**
AFMC is a specialized institution for those who want to serve in the **Armed Forces Medical Services**. In terms of "Pure Research," AIIMS Delhi is higher. In terms of "Discipline and Defence Career," AFMC is #1.

**Q3. What is the fee for MBBS in CMC Vellore?**
CMC Vellore is one of the most affordable non-government colleges. The first-year fees are around ₹50,000, which is significantly lower than other private medical colleges (₹15L+ per year).

**Q4. Are the non-Delhi AIIMS colleges good?**
AIIMS Jodhpur, AIIMS Rishikesh, and AIIMS Bhubaneswar are now becoming "Tier 1" institutes with excellent infrastructure. Newer AIIMS (established after 2019) are still building their hospital patient load.

**Q5. When is the NEET 2026 exam?**
Usually held on the **first Sunday of May**. Registrations typically open in January.
