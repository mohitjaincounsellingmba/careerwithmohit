The countdown for the final attempt of the **Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main 2026** has begun. For lakhs of engineering aspirants across India, Session 2 (April Attempt) is the last opportunity to improve their NTA scores and qualify for the prestigious **JEE Advanced 2026**.

With the examinations scheduled to start in just a few days, here is the complete breakdown of the JEE Main 2026 Session 2 schedule, admit card status, and preparation strategy.

### **JEE Main 2026 Session 2: Official Exam Dates**
The National Testing Agency (NTA) has confirmed that the Session 2 examinations will be conducted between **April 2 and April 9, 2026**.

| Paper Type | Exam Dates |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Paper 1 (B.E. / B.Tech)** | April 2, 4, 5, 6, and 8, 2026 |
| **Paper 2A (B.Arch) & 2B (B.Planning)** | April 7, 2026 |

The exam will be held in two shifts:
*   **Shift 1**: 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM
*   **Shift 2**: 3:00 PM to 6:00 PM

### **Admit Card & City Intimation Slip Status**
*   **City Intimation Slip**: The NTA has already released the exam city intimation slip on **March 21, 2026**. Students can check their allotted city by logging into [jeemain.nta.nic.in](https://jeemain.nta.nic.in).
*   **Admit Card Release Date**: The official JEE Main 2026 Session 2 admit cards are expected to be released by **March 28 or March 30, 2026**. NTA typically releases the admit card 3-4 days before the specific exam date of the candidate.

### **How to Download JEE Main 2026 Admit Card?**
1.  Visit the official NTA JEE website: [jeemain.nta.nic.in](https://jeemain.nta.nic.in).
2.  Click on the link "Candidate Activity Board: Download Admit Card for JEE (Main) 2026 Session 2".
3.  Enter your **Application Number** and **Date of Birth**.
4.  Enter the Security Pin (Captcha) and click on 'Submit'.
5.  Download your admit card and take at least 2 clear printouts (preferebly in color).

### **Last-Minute Preparation Tips for Attempt 2**
If you have already appeared for Session 1, you know your weak areas. Here is how to utilize the remaining time:
1.  **Analyze Session 1 Performance**: Identify the topics that cost you marks in January and prioritize them now.
2.  **Focus on High-Weightage Chapters**: In Physics (Modern Physics, Heat & Thermo), Chemistry (Inorganic, Named Reactions), and Maths (Coordinate Geometry, Calculus).
3.  **Mock Tests**: Solve at least 3-4 full-length NTA mock tests in the exact exam timing (9-12 or 3-6) to build stamina.
4.  **Revision**: Don’t start any new chapter now. Revise your short notes and formulas daily.
