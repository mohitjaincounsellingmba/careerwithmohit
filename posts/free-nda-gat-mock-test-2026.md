## Understanding the NDA GAT Structure

### English (50 Questions – 200 Marks)
English in NDA GAT is designed to test your grammar mastery, vocabulary, and reading comprehension at the Class 12 level. Question types include:
- Spotting errors in grammar
- Fill in the blanks with correct prepositions, conjunctions, and verb forms
- Reading comprehension from passages (100–200 words)
- Ordering of sentences (Para Jumbles)
- Synonyms and Antonyms

### General Knowledge (100 Questions – 400 Marks)
This is divided into 6 sub-sections:

| Sub-Section | Topic Focus |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Physics** | Mechanics, Light, Electricity, Magnetism |
| **Chemistry** | States of matter, Chemical reactions, Metals/Non-metals |
| **General Science** | Biology — human body, diseases, plants |
| **History & Freedom Movement** | Indian freedom struggle, major events/personalities |
| **Geography** | Indian and World geography |
| **Current Events** | Sports, awards, defence news, science developments |

## NDA 2026 Key Eligibility Criteria

- **Age:** 16.5 to 19.5 years (for both NDA I & NDA II)
- **Education:** Passed or appearing in Class 12 (PCM for Army/Navy/Air Force)
- **Gender:** Open to both male and female candidates (from 2021 onwards)
- **Physical Standards:** Minimum height, eyesight, and medical fitness standards apply

## Pro Tips for NDA 2026 Success

- **SSB Interview is Equally Important:** Qualifying the written exam gets you to the Services Selection Board (SSB). Start preparing your personality and leadership qualities simultaneously.
- **Physics is High-Value:** Physics carries the most marks in GK. NCERT Class 11–12 Physics covers 90% of the syllabus.
- **English is Scoring:** 50 English questions are entirely manageable with 2–3 weeks of dedicated practice.
- **Follow Defence News:** Subscribe to PIB Defence Wing, Indian Defence Review, and follow the Ministry of Defence for current affairs.
- **Mathematics (Paper 1):** Don't ignore Maths — it's 300 marks! Focus on Algebra, Trigonometry, and Statistics from Class 11–12.
