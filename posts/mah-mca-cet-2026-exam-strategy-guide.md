## 📝 MAH MCA CET 2026 Exam Pattern

The exam is a 90-minute online test with 100 questions. Unlike NIMCET, there is no weightage factor—every question is worth 2 marks.

| Section | Questions | Marks | Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mathematics & Statistics** | 30 | 60 | 12th Standard NCERT |
| **Logical Reasoning** | 30 | 60 | High (Puzzles, Syllogisms) |
| **Computer Concepts** | 20 | 40 | Operating Systems, Data Structures |
| **English Language** | 20 | 40 | Vocabulary, Comprehension |

**The Strategy:** Since Logic and Math carry equal marks, you must prioritize **Logical Reasoning** as it is often faster to solve than complex math problems.

## 🚀 3-Month MAH MCA CET 2026 Preparation Plan

- **Month 1: Logic & Computer Foundations**
    - Master Syllogisms, Blood Relations, and Coding-Decoding.
    - Revise Binary Arithmetic and Boolean Algebra.
- **Month 2: Math & English Mastery**
    - Focus on **Probability, Statistics, and Set Theory** (High weightage in CET).
    - Solve previous year English papers to understand the vocabulary level.
- **Month 3: The 90-Minute Drill**
    - Take 10 full-length Mock Tests.
    - Focus on speed: You have less than **1 minute per question**.
    - Analyze the cutoff for VJTI (usually 140+ marks).

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is MAH MCA CET harder than NIMCET?**
No. **MAH MCA CET** is easier in terms of math difficulty. However, it is **twice as fast** as NIMCET. It is a test of speed, not deep conceptual depth.

**Q2. Can students outside Maharashtra apply?**
Yes. Through the "All India" category seats. However, you will need a exceptionally high score (usually 99.5+ percentile) to get a top Mumbai/Pune college.

**Q3. Is there negative marking in MAH MCA CET 2026?**
Yes. **0.5 marks** are deducted for every wrong answer.

**Q4. What is the best book for MAH MCA CET logic?**
"A Modern Approach to Verbal & Non-Verbal Reasoning" by R.S. Aggarwal and "Analytical Reasoning" by M. Pandey are the gold standards.

**Q5. When is the best time to start prep?**
Ideally, 4 months before the exam (November/December). If you start in January, you will have to focus only on mocks and high-weightage topics.
