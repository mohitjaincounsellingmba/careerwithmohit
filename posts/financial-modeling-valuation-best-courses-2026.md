## 🏛️ 1. The Core Pillar: Three-Statement Modeling

The foundation of all financial modeling is the ability to link the three primary financial statements such that a change in one automatically flows through to the others.
*   **The Skill:** Mastery of accounting logic (e.g., how an increase in depreciation affects both the P&L and the Cash Flow statement).

---

## 🚀 2. Equity Valuation Techniques

Once the statements are linked, the next step is determining what the company is worth. In 2026, two methods dominate:
1.  **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF):** Projecting future "Free Cash Flows" and discounting them back to today's value. 
2.  **Relative Valuation (Comps):** Comparing the company's P/E or EV/EBITDA ratios with its industry peers.

## 📑 4. Excel Shortcuts: The Secret to IB Success

In an Investment Bank, you are forbidden from using the **Mouse**. You must be able to navigate Excel entirely through the keyboard.
*   **Top Tip for 2026:** Learn the Alt-key sequences for formatting, cell-linking, and trace-dependents. It triples your modeling speed.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. is Financial Modeling different from Accounting?**
Yes. Accounting is about the **Past** (reporting what happened). Financial Modeling is about the **Future** (predicting what will happen).

**Q2. can I learn Financial Modeling for free?**
Yes. Platforms like YouTube (e.g., Aswath Damodaran's channel) and certain open-source university courses offer world-class materials. However, a "Certification" helps in getting through the HR filters.

**Q3. Which software is best for modeling?**
99% of the industry uses **Microsoft Excel**. While Google Sheets is catching up, it lacks the professional processing power and shortcuts required for complex deal modeling.

**Q4. does it require coding (Python/R)?**
Not for traditional Investment Banking. However, for **Quant Finance or High-Frequency Trading**, Python knowledge is becoming mandatory along with Excel.

**Q5. What is the starting salary for a Financial Modeler?**
As an Associate or Junior Analyst, you can expect **₹6L to ₹12L** in Tier-2 firms, climbing to **₹15L+** in top Tier-1 MNCs.
