## Section-Wise Strategy

- **English (30 Q, 20 min):** Error Detection, Cloze Test, Reading Comprehension (1 short RC passage), Para Jumbles, Fill in the Blanks. Aim for 25+ correct attempts.
- **Quantitative Aptitude (35 Q, 20 min):** Number Series, Simplification (highest weightage), Arithmetic Word Problems, and 1 DI set. Focus on simplification/approximation — it yields 10+ quick marks.
- **Reasoning (35 Q, 20 min):** Inequalities, Syllogism, Puzzles (simpler than PO), Blood Relations, and Direction Test. Avoid complex 5-variable puzzles — solve faster inequality and syllogism questions first.

## IBPS Clerk Mains Pattern

After clearing Prelims, the Mains exam consists of:
- General/Financial Awareness (50 Q)
- General English (40 Q)
- Reasoning Ability & Computer Aptitude (50 Q)
- Quantitative Aptitude (50 Q)
- **Total:** 190 Q in 160 Minutes

There is **no interview** for IBPS Clerk. Final merit is based purely on Mains marks plus state/category preference.

## Pro Tips for IBPS Clerk 2026

- **Simplification is Free Marks:** IBPS Clerk places heavy emphasis on Simplification/Approximation (10–15 Qs). These can be solved in 20–30 seconds each. Master BODMAS and go fast.
- **No Complex Puzzles:** Unlike IBPS PO, clerk-level reasoning puzzles are simpler (4–5 variable). There is no need to spend 10 minutes on one set.
- **Mains Involves Financial Awareness:** Start reading Financial/Banking current affairs from Month 1 — it'll be needed for Mains GA section.
- **Apply for Multiple Posts:** Apply for all IBPS processes — Clerk, PO, RRB Officer — simultaneously to maximize chances.
