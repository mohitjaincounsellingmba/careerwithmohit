The **Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET)** is conducted by the **Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE)** to certify the teaching eligibility of candidates for Classes 1–8 in Kendriya Vidyalayas (KVs), Navodaya Vidyalayas (NVs), and central government-funded schools. A CTET certificate is also widely accepted by state governments and many private schools as proof of teaching competency.

## Why CTET Certification Matters

- **Lifetime Validity:** From 2021, CTET certificates are valid for a **lifetime** (previously 7 years). Pass once and stay eligible forever.
- **Central School Eligibility:** KVs have over 1,200 schools across India. NVs operate in every district. A CTET cert is mandatory for teaching roles in these institutions.
- **State Recognition:** Most states accept CTET as an equivalent to their state TET, making this a highly versatile teaching certification.
- **Private School Preference:** Top CBSE-affiliated private schools prefer CTET-certified teachers.

## Section-Wise Strategy

- **CDP (30 Q):** Piaget, Vygotsky, Kohlberg's stages, Gardner's multiple intelligences, constructivism, inclusive education. This section is 100% theory — no calculations.
- **Mathematics (30 Q):** Split between basic math (arithmetic, geometry, fractions) and pedagogy of teaching math. Don't ignore the pedagogy portion — it carries around 15 marks.
- **EVS (30 Q):** Covers plants, animals, family, food, housing, water — basic concepts from Class 3–5 NCERT + pedagogy of teaching EVS.
- **Language 1 & 2 (30+30 Q):** Grammar and comprehension + pedagogy of language teaching. Focus on reading comprehension strategies and second language acquisition theories.
