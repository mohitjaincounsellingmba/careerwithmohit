Higher education is getting more expensive every year, but if you belong to the Jain community, the **Jain International Trade Organisation (JITO)** offers excellent financial support. Through the **JITO Administrative Training Foundation (JATF)**, students can access substantial financial aid to pursue their dreams in India or abroad.

JATF provides two primary higher education financial assistance programs: **JELP** (an interest subsidy program) and **SEED** (a direct interest-free loan). 

This guide covers everything you need to know about both schemes, including eligibility criteria, benefits, and how to apply in 2026.

---

## 1. JITO Education Loan Program (JELP)
The **JELP scheme** does *not* provide the principal loan amount. Instead, it offers an **interest subsidy** on an education loan that a student has already taken from a recognized bank or NBFC. 

This significantly reduces the financial burden of high-interest rates on standard education loans.

### **JELP Eligibility Criteria**
To be eligible for the JELP interest subsidy, students must meet the following conditions:
*   **Community:** Must be a follower of Jain principles.
*   **Age Limit:** Up to 30 years old at the time of application.
*   **Academic Record:** Must have secured **50% or more marks** (or equivalent grade) in all examinations from the 10th standard onwards.
*   **Loan Amount:** The education loan approved by the bank/NBFC must be ₹50,000 or more.

### **JELP Benefits & Key Details**
*   **Subsidy Structure:** Historically, for loans below ₹4 Lakhs, JATF provides up to a **6% interest subsidy**. For loans above ₹4 Lakhs, the subsidy is generally **2%** (rates are subject to annual JATF policy updates).
*   **Registration Deadline:** You must register with JATF within **3 months** of the bank loan sanction date or before March 31st, whichever is earlier.
*   **Claim Period:** Subsidy claims must be renewed and submitted annually between **April 1st and July 31st**.

## 2. SEED (Student Empowerment Education and Development)
Unlike JELP, the **SEED scheme** provides direct financial assistance in the form of an **interest-free loan**. This is primarily aimed at students pursuing Post-Graduation or specific professional courses where graduation is not compulsory.

### **SEED Eligibility Criteria**
*   **Community:** Must be a follower of Jain principles.
*   **Age Limit:** Up to 30 years old.
*   **Academic Record:** Must have an excellent academic track record, securing **60% or more marks** in all exams from the 10th standard onwards.
*   **Financial Need:** The student/family must have already arranged at least **50% of the required total funds**. SEED assists with the remaining shortfall.

### **SEED Benefits & Key Details**
*   **Loan Amount:** Up to **₹2 Lakhs** per student per course.
*   **Repayment:** The loan is completely interest-free, provided it is repaid strictly according to the agreed-upon timeline. Penalties or interest may apply for delayed repayments.
*   **Selection Process:** Given the limited funds, applications are heavily scrutinized, and students will likely be called for a personal interview before approval.

> **Important Note:** A student cannot avail benefits from *both* JELP and SEED for the same course. You must choose the scheme that best fits your financial situation.

## 📄 Required Documents for Application

Whether you are applying for JELP or SEED, keep the following documents ready:
1.  **Proof of Religion:** Certificate from a recognized Jain Sangh/Samaj or a copy of the religion column in your School Leaving Certificate.
2.  **Academic Records:** All mark sheets from 10th standard to your most recent degree.
3.  **Admission Letter:** Official offer/admission letter from the college or university.
4.  **Bank Sanction Letter:** (Mandatory for JELP) Showing the approved loan amount from a bank/NBFC.
5.  **Identity & Address Proof:** Aadhar Card, PAN Card, and Passport (if studying abroad).
6.  **Income Proof:** ITR or Income Certificate of parents/guardian.
7.  **JITO Recommendation:** A highly critical step—your application *must* be recommended by a JITO/JATF Patron, Chapter Chairman, or recognized official.

## 🚀 How to Apply

1.  **Visit the Official Portal:** Go to the official JATF website: [**www.jatf.in**](https://www.jatf.in/)
2.  **Select the Scheme:** Navigate to the 'Projects' section and choose either JELP or SEED.
3.  **Online Application:** Fill out the online registration form carefully and upload all required documents.
4.  **Get a Recommendation:** Co-ordinate with your local JITO chapter for the mandatory recommendation letter.
5.  **Submit & Track:** Track your application status directly through the JATF portal.
