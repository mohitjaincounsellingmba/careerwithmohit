For candidates who have qualified JEE Main or JEE Advanced 2026, the JoSAA counselling process is the most critical step in securing a seat in their dream college.

With the JoSAA 2026 session expected to begin in **June 2026**, here is a comprehensive guide to the schedule, registration process, and key rules.

### **Step-by-Step JoSAA 2026 Counselling Process**
1.  **Online Registration**: Visit [josaa.nic.in](https://josaa.nic.in) and log in using your JEE Main application number and password.
2.  **Choice Filling**: This is the most vital part. You must list your preferred combinations of Institutes and Branches in decreasing order of preference. 
    *   *Tip: Fill as many choices as possible to avoid losing out in the final rounds.*
3.  **Mock Seat Allotment**: JoSAA releases mock results to show where you might get admission based on your current choices. Use this to re-order your list.
4.  **Seat Allotment Rounds**: JoSAA typically conducts 6 rounds. After each round, if you are allotted a seat, you must choose one of the following:
    *   **Freeze**: You are happy with the seat and want to secure it.
    *   **Float**: You accept the seat but want to wait for a better college/branch in the next round.
    *   **Slide**: You accept the seat but want a better branch within the same institute.
5.  **Fee Payment & Document Upload**: Once you accept a seat, you must pay the **Seat Acceptance Fee (SAF)** and upload documents for online verification.

### **Documents Required for JoSAA 2026**
Ensure you have scanned copies of these documents ready:
*   JEE Main/Advanced Admit Card & Scorecard
*   Class 10th & 12th Marksheets (Date of Birth proof)
*   Category Certificate (OBC-NCL/SC/ST/EWS) - *Must be issued after April 1, 2026*
*   Medical Certificate (in JoSAA format)
*   Cancelled Cheque or Bank Passbook copy
*   Photo ID Proof

### **Expert Tips for a Successful JoSAA Counselling**
*   **Don't Wait for the Last Date**: Server issues are common on the final day of choice locking.
*   **Check Placement Records**: Before opting for a newer IIIT or GFTI, research their placement trends and campus infrastructure.
*   **Understand CSAB**: If you don't get a seat in JoSAA, you can participate in **CSAB Special Rounds** for vacant seats in NITs/IIITs/GFTIs.
