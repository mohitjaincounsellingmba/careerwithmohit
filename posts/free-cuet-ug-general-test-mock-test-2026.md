## Why CUET UG Replaced Board Marks for Admissions

Before CUET, DU and other central universities used Class 12 board marks (cutoffs reaching 99–100%) for admission. This was deeply unfair to students from different boards. CUET creates a level playing field where every student, regardless of their board, competes on a common test.

Key reasons CUET matters:
- **100+ Central Universities:** All 44 central universities now mandatorily participate in CUET.
- **Domain + General Test:** Most programmes require 1 domain test (e.g., Mathematics, Physics) + General Test.
- **No Board-Based Cutoffs:** Your CUET score determines your admission, not your 12th percentage.

## Section-Wise Strategy

- **GK & Current Affairs (20 Q):** Focus on last 6 months of news — government schemes, appointments, awards, sports championships, and international summits. This is the purest memory-based section.
- **Mental Ability (20 Q):** Includes series, analogies, coding-decoding, direction test, and Non-Verbal Reasoning (mirror images, pattern completion). NCERT-level logical thinking is sufficient.
- **Numerical Ability (20 Q):** Arithmetic (Percentage, Profit & Loss, Ratio, Speed-Distance), Data Tables, and Bar Graph interpretation. Class 10 Math level.

## Pro Tips for CUET UG General Test 2026

- **Manage Time Aggressively:** 60 questions in 60 minutes = 1 minute per question. With the +4/-1 scheme, accuracy over speed is the winning strategy.
- **Target 50-55 Correct Answers:** A score of 50+ out of 60 (200/240 marks) is typically competitive for most DU programmes.
- **Don't Skip GK:** Students often underestimate GK thinking it's unpredictable — but 70% of CUET GK questions repeat from prior 6-month current affairs. A structured current affairs tracker helps massively.
- **Domain Tests Matter More:** General Test is one component. Focus equally on your domain subject (PCM/Commerce/Arts) test for stronger programme-specific scores.
