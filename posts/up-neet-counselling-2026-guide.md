## 🏗️ Step-by-Step UP NEET Counselling Process

### Step 1: Online Registration
Register on the official **DGME UP** (Directorate General of Medical Education) website (**upneet.gov.in**). You will need to pay a non-refundable registration fee (approx ₹2,000).

### Step 2: Security Deposit (Crucial)
This is where many students make mistakes. To participate in choice filling, you MUST pay a security deposit:
- **Government Seats only:** ₹30,000
- **Private Medical (MBBS):** ₹2,00,000
- **Private Dental (BDS):** ₹1,00,000

*Note: This amount is refundable if you are not allotted a seat or if you take admission, but forfeited if you are allotted a seat and don't join in later rounds.*

### Step 3: Document Verification
UP used to have offline verification at "Nodal Centers," but it has recently moved to an online/hybrid model. Keep your NEET admit card, Marksheets, Domicile, and Category certificates ready.

### Step 4: Merit List & Choice Filling
The DGME will release a state merit list. Based on your rank, you will fill your preferences for colleges. 
- **Pro Tip:** In UP, once you are allotted a seat in Round 1, you cannot participate in further rounds unless you give up the first seat (with certain penalties).

## 🚀 The UP Advantage: The "Open State"
Unlike many South Indian states that reserve private medical seats for their own residents, **Uttar Pradesh is an OPEN STATE**. This means a student from Delhi, Haryana, or Bihar can apply for private medical seats in UP just like a local student. This makes Lucknow and Noida the most popular destinations for management quota seats.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. can I get a private medical seat in UP without NEET?**
ABSOLUTELY NOT. Any agent promising direct admission without NEET is a scammer. You must be NEET-qualified and must participate in the official counselling.

**Q2. is the security deposit refundable?**
Yes. If you are not allotted any seat, the deposit is returned to your bank account. If you take admission, it is usually adjusted in your college fees.

**Q3. Do I need a UP Domicile for private medical colleges?**
No. UP domicile is only required for the **85% State Quota in Government colleges**. Private medical seats are open to all Indian citizens.

**Q4. What is the fee for private MBBS in Uttar Pradesh?**
Tuition fees usually range from ₹10.5 Lakhs to ₹16 Lakhs per year. Including hostel and miscellaneous charges, the total annual cost is around ₹15.0L - ₹18.5L.

**Q5. When is the Round 2 of UP NEET counselling?**
Usually 15-20 days after the Round 1 result. Round 2 is often when the "Category-wise" shift happens and some lower-tier private seats become available.
