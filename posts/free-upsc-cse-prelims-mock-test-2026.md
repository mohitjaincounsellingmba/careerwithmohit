## What Makes UPSC Prelims Unique?

- **No Straightforward Questions:** UPSC rarely asks direct factual questions. Instead, it asks comparative, application, or "which of the following is CORRECT/INCORRECT" type questions.
- **Statement-Based Format:** A significant portion of questions (40–50%) involve evaluating multiple statements (I, II, III) and picking the correct combination.
- **Interdisciplinary Nature:** A single question might link Ancient History with Environment, or connect Polity with recent Cabinet decisions.
- **CSAT (Paper 2):** GS Paper 2 is a qualifying paper (minimum 33%). Our focus here is GS Paper 1, which determines your Prelims rank.

## Section-Wise Strategy

- **History & Culture:** NCERT Class 6–12 is mandatory. Focus on Modern Indian History (freedom movement, key personalities) and Art & Architecture of ancient India.
- **Polity:** Read Laxmikanth's Indian Polity thoroughly. Constitutional amendments, committees, and landmark supreme court cases are crucial.
- **Geography & Environment:** NCERT Physical Geography (Class 11) + environmental topics from The Hindu and Shankar IAS notes.
- **Economy & Current Affairs:** Follow Budget 2026, Economic Survey, and 12 months of current affairs from a reliable source (The Hindu/PIB).
