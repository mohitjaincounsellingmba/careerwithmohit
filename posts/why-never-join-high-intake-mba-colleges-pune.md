Choosing an MBA college is more than just looking at the brand name or the "Average Package" on a shiny brochure. One of the most critical factors that students often ignore—until it’s too late—is the **Batch Intake.**

At **CareerWithMohit**, I speak to hundreds of students every year who feel "lost" in the crowd. In Pune, many famous B-schools have expanded their seats to 600, 800, or even 1,000+ students. 

While these colleges have great campuses, here is the unfiltered reality: **Joining a high-intake MBA college can be a massive risk to your career.**

---

### **The Hidden Risks of High Intake (600+ Students)**

#### **1. The "1,000 vs 5" Math**
If a college has 1,000 students and a top recruiter like Deloitte or HUL visits, they aren't going to hire 100 people. They still have a quota of 5–10 students. 
*   **Low Intake (120-180 students):** You compete with 18 people for one seat.
*   **High Intake (1,000 students):** You compete with 100+ people for that same seat.
Your probability of landing a dream role drops significantly the moment the intake increases.

#### **2. Diluted Individual Attention**
In a batch of 120, the Placement Director likely knows your name, your strengths, and your interests. In a batch of 1,000, you are just a **Serial Number.** There is no personalized grooming, no one-on-one mock interviews, and no specialized guidance for your specific career path.

#### **3. The "Creamy Layer" vs. The Rest**
Most high-intake colleges in Pune use the "Top 10%" to market their brand. 
*   The top 50–100 students get 12–15 LPA packages.
*   The remaining 900 students often end up in "mass recruiter" sales roles paying 4–6 LPA.
**But remember: You all pay the same high fees (₹10 Lakh+).**

#### **4. Infrastructure Strain**
Imagine 1,000 students trying to use one library, one computer lab, or one canteen at the same time. The quality of learning and campus life inevitably takes a hit when the crowd exceeds the capacity.

### **Pool Placements: The Double-Edged Sword**
Many of these high-intake colleges use **"Centralized Placements"** or **"Pool Placements."** This means students from 4 to 5 different campuses descend upon one location for interviews. 

As I explained in our [Pool Placement Warning Guide](/posts/why-never-join-pool-placement-colleges-mba-pgdm), this often benefits the college (lower cost) but hurts the student (higher competition).

### **What Should You Do?**

If you don't have a 95+ percentile to get into [Top MBA Colleges in Pune](/posts/top-mba-colleges-pune) like SIBM or PUMBA (which have disciplined intakes), look for:

1.  **Niche B-Schools:** Colleges that maintain a strict intake of 120–180 students.
2.  **Specialization-Specific ROI:** Look for colleges where the intake for *your* specialization (like Finance or Business Analytics) is small.
3.  **Direct Company Tie-ups:** Choose colleges that have dedicated independent placement cells for their specific campus.

### **Final Verdict**
A high intake isn't always "bad," but it requires you to be **extraordinarily competitive.** If you are an "average" student looking for personal growth, you will likely get lost in these massive batches.

### **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**1. Is Balaji University Pune good for average students?**
Balaji has a great legacy, but with 1,000+ students, the competition is fierce. If you aren't ready to work 12–14 hours a day and compete with 1,000 peers, you might find it difficult.

**2. Which college has the highest ROI in Pune?**
**PUMBA** (Pune University) is the winner here. It has a controlled intake and very low fees compared to the private giants.

**3. Is 600 students considered "high intake"?**
Yes. In the world of MBA, any batch size above 300 for a single campus is considered high and requires a massive corporate network to place everyone fairly.

**4. How can I check the real intake of a college?**
Check the **AICTE Extension of Approval (EoA)** letter on the college website. They are legally required to disclose their approved intake.
