## 🏛️ The Eastern Giant: WBNUJS

### 1. West Bengal National University of Juridical Sciences (WBNUJS)
Consistently ranked India’s #3 Law school. NUJS is famous for its "Corporate Ready" graduates and for hosting some of the most competitive moot court teams in the world.
- **Top Recruiters:** Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas, Shardul Amarchand Mangaldas, Trilegal.
- **Admission Strategy:** 100% focus on securing a top-150 rank in **CLAT UG**.

## 🚀 Legacy and Value Hubs: Heritage & CU

### 2. Heritage Law College
The most trusted private law name in West Bengal. Heritage offers a balanced curriculum with a strong focus on preparing students for the 2026 corporate law firm cycle.

### 3. South Calcutta Law College
One of the most prestigious colleges affiliated with the University of Calcutta. It has the best alumni network in the Calcutta High Court.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is WBNUJS better than NALSAR Hyderabad?**
They are practically equal. WBNUJS is often preferred by students from North and East India due to its proximity and its very strong placements in the "Big Seven" firms.

**Q2. Does University of Calcutta (CU) have an entrance exam?**
Previously CU had its separate entrance, but for many Law colleges, admission is now primarily based on **12th-grade merit or WB-state level admission tests**. Always check the latest CU Gazette for the 2026 intake.

**Q3. What is the fee for Law in St. Xavier's Kolkata?**
St. Xavier's University (New Town) charges around ₹1 Lakh to ₹1.5 Lakhs per year for its 5-year integrated law courses.

**Q4. Can I practice in other states after Law from Kolkata?**
YES. A law degree from any BCI-recognized college in Kolkata allows you to practice anywhere in India after clearing the All India Bar Exam (AIBE).

**Q5. When is the Kolkata Law admission 2026 starting?**
Registrations for merit-based seats usually open in **June** after the HS and CBSE results.
