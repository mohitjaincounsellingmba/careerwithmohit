## Why UPSSSC PET is Different from All Other Exams

The **Graph & Table Analysis** section (20 questions, 20 marks) is the signature feature of UPSSSC PET that distinguishes it from all SSC/IBPS exams:

- Candidates must interpret complex data tables (multiple rows and columns)
- Bar graph and pie chart comparisons across multiple years or categories
- Questions test reading speed and data extraction — not mathematical computation
- This section is often where unprepared candidates lose the most marks

In addition, PET tests both **Hindi and English** in the same paper — a unique bilingual requirement not found in most central government exams.

## Section-Wise Preparation Guide

### History & Geography (20 Q)
Same coverage as SSC GK but with UP focus:
- **History:** Ancient India (Magadh, Maurya, Gupta), Freedom Movement, Partition
- **Geography:** Physical features of India (rivers, mountains, plains), world capitals/currencies, climate zones
- **UP Special:** Agra-Mathura heritage, Varanasi's cultural importance, Lucknow's Awadhi culture

### Hindi & English (15 Q)
- **Hindi:** Fill in the blanks (kaarak, kaal, vachan), synonyms/antonyms in Hindi, one-word substitution, proverbs
- **English:** Basic grammar (tense, preposition, articles), simple vocabulary (synonyms, antonyms), short reading comprehension

### Mathematics & Reasoning (15 Q)
- **Maths:** Percentage, Ratio, Average, Simple Interest, Time-Work (Class 10 level)
- **Reasoning:** Number series, analogy, coding-decoding, direction test (5–7 Q each)

### Graph & Table Analysis (20 Q) — Most Unique Section!
Practice interpreting:
- **Double Bar Graphs:** Year-wise comparison of two variables
- **Pie Charts:** Percentage distribution analysis
- **Line Graphs:** Trend identification across multiple years
- **Complex Tables:** Multi-row, multi-column data with derived calculations (sums, differences, averages)

**Strategy:** Read the graph/table title first, identify the variables, then answer questions in sequence. Never read all data before questions — answer what is asked.

### Current Affairs & General Studies (30 Q)
- National news: Last 6 months (government schemes, appointments, economic data)
- **UP Current Affairs:** Yogi government schemes, new district/airport inaugurations, UP infrastructure projects
- Science & Technology: Recent inventions, ISRO missions, WHO/health news
- Economy: Budget highlights, GDP data, inflation figures

## Pro Tips for UPSSSC PET 2026

- **Graph Analysis is Your Differentiator:** Most candidates neglect this section. 2 hours of daily graph/table practice for 3 weeks will yield 17–18 marks out of 20 — a massive advantage.
- **Read Amar Ujala or Dainik Jagran Daily:** Both cover UP-specific news comprehensively and are available in Hindi — aligned with the bilingual requirement of PET.
- **Hindi Grammar is Easy Marks:** Even non-Hindi-medium students can learn essential Hindi grammar rules in 1–2 weeks. Don't leave this section unprepared.
- **Target 85+ Marks:** With normalisation across exam shifts, scoring 85+ is generally safe for advanced processes like Lekhpal mains.
- **PET Score Card Validity:** Each UPSSSC PET score is typically valid for 1 year. Use your scorecard efficiently during that window.
