## The MHCET MBA Admission Process (CAP)

Admissions to MBA/MMS programs in colleges accepting MAH CET are administered through the **Centralized Admission Process (CAP)** conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra.

Even if you hold credible scores from CAT, CMAT, MAT, or ATMA, registering for the MAH-MBA/MMS CET CAP rounds is mandatory to secure seats in these participating colleges.

### The CAP 2026 Step-by-Step Flow:

1. **CAP Registration:** After CET results are announced, candidates must register online on the official State CET Cell website.
2. **Document Verification:** You'll need to submit and verify your educational documents, domicile certificate (if applicable), and category/caste documents at designated E-Scrutiny centers or offline Application Receipt Centres (ARCs).
3. **Display of Merit List:** The CET Cell publishes a Provisional Merit List, followed by a Final Merit List based on the candidates' CET scores and academic profiles.
4. **Option Form Filling:** Candidates must submit an order of preference (Option Form) for colleges and courses. *Pro Tip: Always put JBIMS and SIMSREE at the top!*
5. **Seat Allotment (CAP Rounds 1, 2, and 3):** Based on merit, category, and filled options, seats are allocated. 
6. **Freeze/Float & Reporting:** If you receive your top choice, your seat is auto-frozen, and you must report to the college. If you receive a lower preference, you can choose "Float" to upgrade in the next round. Once satisfied, pay the seat acceptance fee and report to the allotted institute to complete your admission.

---

### Important Considerations for 2026 Applicants

*   **MBA vs. PGDM:** The CAP process primarily allocates seats for the University-affiliated **MMS/MBA** programs. Many top autonomous colleges (like Welingkar or SIES) also run autonomous PGDM programs which might have separate institute-level admission processes alongside CAP. Always check individual college websites.
*   **Domicile Advantage:** Maharashtra State (MS) candidates have a significantly higher seat allocation quota (typically 85%) compared to Outside Maharashtra State (OMS) / All India candidates (15%).
